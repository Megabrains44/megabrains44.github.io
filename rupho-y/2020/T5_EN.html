Electrons in a magnetic field

Y20 (2020) — English translation

Recent advances in nanotechnology have made it possible to create “artificial atoms,” so-called quantum dots. These are nanometer structures that, due to their optical and electronic properties, have a variety of applications: from quantum computers, solar cells to imaging of living cells. In this problem, we will consider a model of electron motion in a quantum dot.

Let us consider the movement of two electrons (mass $m$ and charge $q=-e$) in a plane perpendicular to the lines of a uniform magnetic induction field $\vec B=B\vec k$. Electrons can be considered material points between which there is only a Coulomb interaction. Relativistic effects can be neglected.

Let the electrons be at a distance $d$ from each other at the initial moment. The electrons are given velocities equal in magnitude but opposite in direction, such that the distance between them does not change with further movement.
