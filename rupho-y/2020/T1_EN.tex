\title{\textbf{Polytropic hat-trick}}
\author{\normalsize Y20 (2020) --- English translation}
\date{}
\begin{document}
\maketitle

Once Lord Kelvin carried out a cycle of a heat engine, the working fluid of which was one mole of an ideal gas with adiabatic index $\gamma$. The cycle consists of three polytropic processes, one of which is isobaric, and the product of the exponents of the other two polytropes $13$ and $23$ was equal to $n_{13}n_{23}=-1$, where $1$ and $2$ are the isobar points with minimum and maximum temperatures, respectively, and $3$ is the intersection point of the polytrope $13$ and $23$. The temperature at point $3$ satisfies the relation $T_3^2=T_1T_2$. $T_1=100~\text{К}$, $T_2=400~\text{К}$, $\gamma=1.5$.

\problembox{A1}{Find the work $A$ of the gas in the cycle.}{3.50}

\problembox{A2}{Find the efficiency $\eta$ of the cycle.}{1.50}
