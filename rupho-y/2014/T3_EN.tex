\title{\textbf{Dove prism}}
\author{\normalsize Y14 (2014) --- English translation}
\date{}
\begin{document}
\maketitle

In an isosceles rectangular glass prism, the base $АВ$ and side face $ВС$ are smooth, and the face $АС$ is matte. The base $АВ$ of the prism stands on the newspaper. The refractive index of the prism is $n = 1.6$.

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.72\textwidth]{T3_EN_assets/fig1.jpg}
    \caption{Figure 1.}
\end{figure}

\problembox{A1}{What part of the $\alpha$ text is seen by an observer looking at the text through the edge $ВС$?}{0}

The following questions consider a beam of rays incident on a prism parallel to the face $АВ$. To reverse (invert) an image, a Dove prism is often used, which is a truncated rectangular isosceles prism.

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.72\textwidth]{T3_EN_assets/fig2.jpg}
    \caption{Figure 2.}
\end{figure}

\problembox{A2}{Determine the height $h$ of the prism if the length of its base is $L = 10~\text{cm}$. The prism should wrap around a beam of maximum cross-section and not contain “non-functioning” glass.}{0}

\problembox{A3}{At what values ​​of the refractive index $n$ will no part of the light escape from the face $АВ$?}{0}
