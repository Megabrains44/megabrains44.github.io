\title{\textbf{Increasing field}}
\author{\normalsize Y14 (2014) --- English translation}
\date{}
\begin{document}
\maketitle

Inside a long solenoid of radius $R$, the magnetic field strength increases with time according to the linear law $B_\text{in} = \alpha \cdot t$. Outside, in a plane perpendicular to the axis of the solenoid and passing through its middle, we can assume that the field is $B_\text{out}=0$.

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.72\textwidth]{T8_3_EN_assets/fig1.jpg}
    \caption{Figure 1.}
\end{figure}

\problembox{A1}{Determine the electric field strength $E$ inside and outside the solenoid as a function of $r$, the distance from the solenoid axis. Construct a graph of $E(r)$.}{4.00}
