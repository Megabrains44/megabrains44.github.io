\begin{solcard}{A1}{Let the electron begin to move with zero initial speed from the point $C$. Show that in this case $x(t) = 0$.}{0.30}

At the initial moment, the electron acquires speed along the $Oy$ axis, along the electric field. This leads to the emergence of a magnetic force along $Oz$ and the appearance of a velocity component $v_z$. The magnetic force associated with $v_z$ is parallel to the $Oy$ axis. Thus, in the process of further movement, the forces acting on the electron lie in the $Oyz$ plane, from which follows $x(t) = 0$.

\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{A2}{For the described case, write down Newton’s second law in projection on the $y$, $z$ axes, from which you will obtain expressions for $\ddot{y}$, $\ddot{z}$. Expressions may contain $\dot{y}$, $\dot{z}$, $y$, $z$, $U$, $I$, as well as geometric characteristics of the system.}{0.50}

In a flat configuration, the electric field is uniform and equal to $$E_y = \frac{\varphi_К - \varphi_А}{h} = -\frac{U}{h}.$$Magnetic field проwater проwater for/at движении electronа в плоскости $Oyz$ vaporаллельно $Ox$ equals (for example, from теоремы о циркуляции) $$B_x = -\frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi(y+r_0)}.$$ Taking into account отрицательности signа chargeа electronа we get уравнения движения: $$\begin{cases} m \ddot{y} = -eE_y - e\dot{z}B_x \\ m\ddot{z} = e\dot{y}B_x \end{cases}$$Substituting expressions for the fields, we get the answer.

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$\begin{cases} \ddot{y} = \dfrac{e U}{m h} + \dfrac{\mu_0 e I}{2\pi m (y+r_0)}\dot{z} \qquad  (1)\\ \ddot{z} = -\dfrac{\mu_0 e I}{2 \pi m (y+r_0)}\dot{y} \qquad \qquad \ \, (2) \end{cases}$$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{A3}{Obtain a differential equation for $y$ of the form $$\ddot{y} = \alpha - \beta \frac{\ln \left(1+\frac{y}{r_0}\right)}{1+\frac{y}{r_0}},$$ where $\alpha$, $\beta$ – константы. Find $\alpha$, $\beta$. The answer may contain all previously entered quantities and constants.}{1.00}

Multiplying the second equation by $dt$, we arrive at the relation $$d\dot{z} = -\dfrac{\mu_0 e I}{2 \pi m (y+r_0)}dy.$$Integrating: $$\dot{z} = -\dfrac{\mu_0 e I}{2 \pi m} \int \frac{dy'}{y'+r_0} = -\dfrac{\mu_0 e I}{2 \pi m} \, \ln\left(1+\frac{y}{r_0}\right)+C.$$C учётом начальных условий $\dot{z} = 0$ for/at $y = 0$, then $C = 0$, and $$\dot{z} = -\dfrac{\mu_0 e I}{2 \pi m} \, \ln\left(1+\frac{y}{r_0}\right).$$ Постановка поrayенного выражения в $(1)$ gives $$\ddot{y} = \dfrac{e U}{m h} - \frac{1}{r_0}\left( \dfrac{\mu_0 e I}{2\pi m}\right)^2 \, \dfrac{\ln\left(1+\frac{y}{r_0}\right)}{1+\frac{y}{r_0}}. \tag{3}$$From here we get the answer.

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$\alpha = \frac{eU}{mh} \\ \ \\ \beta = \frac{1}{r_0}\left( \dfrac{\mu_0 e I}{2\pi m}\right)^2$$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{A4}{Find the maximum value of the heater current $I_{кр}$ at which the electron in question reaches the anode.}{0.80}

After multiplying by $\dot{y}$, the equation $(3)$ is transformed to the form $$\frac{\dot{y}^2}{2} = \int \left(\dfrac{e U}{m h} - \frac{1}{r_0}\left( \dfrac{\mu_0 e I}{2\pi m}\right)^2 \, \dfrac{\ln\left(1+\frac{y}{r_0}\right)}{1+\frac{y}{r_0}}\right) dy.$$ Интеграл во втором слагаемом: $$\int \dfrac{\ln\left(1+\frac{y}{r_0}\right)}{1+\frac{y}{r_0}} dy = r_0 \int \ln\left(1+\frac{y}{r_0}\right)d\left(\ln\left(1+\frac{y}{r_0}\right)\right) = \frac{r_0}{2} \ln^2\left(1+\frac{y}{r_0}\right) + C.$$ Taking into account начальных условий $\dot{y} = 0$ for/at $y = 0$, then $C = 0$ , and $$\frac{\dot{y}^2}{2} = \frac{eUy}{mh} - \frac{1}{2} \left( \dfrac{\mu_0 e I}{2\pi m}\right)^2 \ln^2\left(1+\frac{y}{r_0}\right).$$ Condition достижения electronом анода: $$\dot{y}(h) = 0, \\ 0 = \frac{eU}{m} - \frac{1}{2} \left(\dfrac{\mu_0 e I_{кр}}{2\pi m}\right)^2 \ln^2\left(1+\frac{h}{r_0}\right).$$Hence we get answer for $I_{cr}$.

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$I_{кр} = \sqrt{\frac{8 U m}{e}} \frac{\pi}{\mu_0} \frac{1}{\ln\left(1+\frac{h}{r_0}\right)}$$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{A5}{Let the electron start moving with zero initial speed from the point $C$ in the cylindrical lamp configuration. In this case, find the maximum value of the heater current $I_{кр}$ at which the electron reaches the anode.}{1.20}

Similar to the previous situation, it is shown that the movement will occur in the $Oyz$ plane if the axes are introduced in the same way as in the figure for a plane configuration.


Method 1


We will consider the movement in coordinates $(r, z)$, where $r$ is the distance from the system axis to the point. Let's calculate the electric field using Gauss's theorem. Due to the absence of volumetric charges $$E_r  \cdot r = \mbox{const}. \quad \Rightarrow \quad E_r= \frac{A}{r}, \\ U = -\int \limits_{r_0}^{R_0} E_r dr = -A \ln\left(\frac{R_0}{r_0}\right), \\ E_r = -\frac{U}{r \ln \left(\frac{R_0}{r_0}\right)}.$$ Magnetic field: $$B_x = -\frac{\mu_0 I}{2 \pi r}.$$ Then уравнения движения выглядят следующим обсом: $$\begin{cases} \ddot{r} = -\dfrac{e U}{m r \ln \left(\frac{R_0}{r_0}\right)} + \dfrac{\mu_0 e I}{2\pi m (y+r_0)}\dot{z} \\ \ddot{z} = -\dfrac{\mu_0 e I}{2 \pi m (y+r_0)}\dot{r} \end{cases}$$ Аналогичным обсом после интегрирования второго уравнения, подстановки в первое and повторного интегрирования we get equation на $I_{cr}$: $$\frac{e U}{m} -  \frac{1}{2} \left(\frac{\mu_0 e I_{кр}}{2 \pi m} \right)^2 \ln^2 \left(\frac{R_0}{r_0}\right).$$ From here we get the answer.


Method 2


Let's consider the equations of motion from point A2, without substituting the specific values of $E_y$ and $B_x$: $$\begin{cases} \ddot{y} = -\dfrac{e}{m} \left(E_y(y) + \dot{z}B_x(y) \right) \\ \ddot{z} = \dfrac{e}{m} \dot{y}B_x(y) \end{cases}.$$ Taking into account начальных условий from второго уравнения we get: $$\dot{z} = \frac{e}{m} \int \limits_0^y B_x(\xi) d\xi.$$Substituting в первое equation: $$\ddot{y} = -\frac{e}{m}\left(E_y(y) + \frac{e}{m} B_x(y) \int \limits_0^y B_x(\xi) d\xi\right).$$Домножим на $\dot{y}$ and проинтегрируем taking into account начальных условий: $$\frac{\dot{y}^2}{2} = -\frac{e}{m} \int \limits_0^y E_y(\eta) d\eta - \frac{e^2}{m^2} \int \limits_0^y B_x(\eta) d\eta \int \limits_0^\eta B_x(\xi) d\xi = \\ = \frac{e}{m}\left(\varphi(y) - \varphi(0)\right) - \frac{e^2}{m^2} \int \limits_0^y B_x(\eta) d\eta \int \limits_0^\eta B_x(\xi) d\xi.$⟦1 0⟧$\frac{e}{m} \int \limits_0^h B_x(\eta) d\eta \int \limits_0^\eta B_x(\xi) d\xi = \varphi(y) - \varphi(0) = U.$$Thus, condition не зависит от расlimitения поля $E_y$, но только от сности potentialов $U$. Since расlimitение магнитного поля в cylinderandческой and в плоской системе совпадают, то and answer for $I_{cr}$ (с точностью до подстановки $R_0 = r_0 + h$) remains the same.


Note. It can be shown that $$\int \limits_0^h B_x(\eta) d\eta \int \limits_0^\eta B_x(\xi) d\xi = \frac{1}{2} \left(\int\limits_0^h B_x(\xi)d\xi)\right)^2.$$ Thus, condition на достижение electronом анода: $$\int\limits_0^h B_x(\xi)d\xi = \sqrt{\frac{2mU}{e}}.$$ Интересный факт заключается в том, что найденное condition зависит только от значений оlimitённых интегралов электрического and магнитного поля по $y$, but not from a specific field distribution.


It can be shown that


$$\int \limits_0^h B_x(\eta) d\eta \int \limits_0^\eta B_x(\xi) d\xi = \frac{1}{2} \left(\int\limits_0^h B_x(\xi)d\xi)\right)^2.$$


Thus, the condition for the electron to reach the anode is:


$$\int\limits_0^h B_x(\xi)d\xi = \sqrt{\frac{2mU}{e}}.$$


An interesting fact is that the found condition depends only on the values ​​of certain integrals of the electric and magnetic field over $y$, but not on the specific field distribution.

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$I_{кр} = \sqrt{\frac{8 U m}{e}} \frac{\pi}{\mu_0} \frac{1}{\ln\left(\frac{R_0}{r_0}\right)}$$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{B1}{Derive a differential equation relating $\varphi(y)$ and $\rho(y)$.}{0.30}

Method 2

In the one-dimensional case this transforms to the form

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$\varphi''(y) = -\frac{\rho(y)}{\varepsilon_0}$$
\end{solcard}
