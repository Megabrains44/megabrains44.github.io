\begin{solcard}{A1}{Write down a system of laws of conservation of mass and momentum for the volume of gas passing through the wave front of the shock wave. Determine from it the speed of movement of the wave front $c$.}{1.00}

Let us move to a reference frame that moves with the velocity of the shock wave front $c$. In this CO, a gas with pressure $p_0$ and density $\rho_0$ impinges on a stationary front, and after the front moves with a certain speed $u$. Throughout the problem we will use this reference system. In time $dt$, mass $\rho_0  c dt$ passes through a unit area of the flow, and mass $\rho_1 u dt$ exits, therefore the law of conservation of mass $$ \rho_0 c = \rho_1 u. $$Momentum можно поrayandть, домножив массу на соответствующую velocity. At the same time momentum gasа изменяется for/at прохождении волнового фронта за счет сности давлений на quantity $(p_0 - p_1) dt$, therefore $$ (\rho_1 u^2 - \rho_0 c^2 )dt = (p_0 - p_1 )dt, $$and finally $$ p_0 + \rho_0 c^2 = p_1 + \rho_1 u^2. $$Eliminating velocity $u$ с помощью закона сохранения массы: $$ u = c \frac{\rho_0}{\rho_1} $$and we get $$ p_0 + \rho_0 c^2 = p_1 + \frac{\rho_0^2}{\rho_1} c^2, $$hence velocity волны $$ c = \sqrt{\frac{p_1 - p_0}{\rho_1 - \rho_0} \frac{\rho_1}{\rho_0}}. $$

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$ \rho_0 c = \rho_1 u, \quad p_0 + \rho_0 c^2 = p_1 + \frac{\rho_0^2}{\rho_1} c^2, \quad c = \sqrt{\frac{p_1 - p_0}{\rho_1 - \rho_0} \frac{\rho_1}{\rho_0}}. $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{A2}{Write down the law of conservation of energy for an element of the volume of gas passing through the wave front. Obtain from it an additional equation relating the parameters $p_0$, $\rho_0$, $p_1$, $\rho_1$, the speed of movement of the wave front $c$ and the adiabatic exponent $\gamma$.}{1.00}

Let us consider the mass $dm$ of gas crossing the boundary. Its kinetic energy changes by $$ \Delta E_{\text{к}} = \left( \frac{u^2}{2}  -\frac{c^2}{2}\right) dm, $$внутренняя energy на $$ \Delta U =  C_V \frac{dm}{\mu} (T_1 - T_0 )= C_V \frac{dm}{R} \left( \frac{p_1}{\rho_1} - \frac{p_0}{\rho_0}\right). $$Here $C_V$ – молярная heat capacity gasа for/at постоянном volumeе, а temperature выражена via/through pressure с помощью уравнения состояния идеального gasа. Над gasом совершена work $$ A =p_0 \frac{dm}{\rho_0} -p_1 \frac{dm}{\rho_1}. $$Therefore energy conservation law \textbackslash{}begin{align*} A \&= \textbackslash{}Delta E_{\textbackslash{}text{к}} + \textbackslash{}Delta U, \textbackslash{}\textbackslash{} p_0 \textbackslash{}frac{dm}{\textbackslash{}rho_0} -p_1 \textbackslash{}frac{dm}{\textbackslash{}rho_1}\& = \textbackslash{}left( \textbackslash{}frac{u^2}{2}  -\textbackslash{}frac{c^2}{2}\textbackslash{}right) dm + C_V \textbackslash{}frac{dm}{R} \textbackslash{}left( \textbackslash{}frac{p_1}{\textbackslash{}rho_1} - \textbackslash{}frac{p_0}{\textbackslash{}rho_0}\textbackslash{}right). \textbackslash{}end{align*} Сократим на массу and перенесем все terms, относящиеся к gasу до прохождения волнового фронта в одну сторону: $$ \frac{p_0}{\rho_0} + \frac{C_V}{R} \frac{p_0}{\rho_0} + \frac{c^2}{2} = \frac{p_1}{\rho_1} + \frac{C_V}{R} \frac{p_1}{\rho_1} + \frac{u^2}{2}. $$Это соratio – equation Бернулли, в котором внутренняя energy gasа выражена via/through pressure and density. Note that $$ \frac{C_V}{R} + 1 = \frac{C_P}{R} = \frac{C_P}{C_P-C_V} = \frac{\gamma}{\gamma - 1}, $$а also substituting expression for скорости $u$ via/through velocity волнового фронта $c$ and we get

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$ \frac{\gamma}{\gamma - 1} \frac{p_0}{\rho_0} + \frac{c^2}{2} = \frac{\gamma}{\gamma - 1} \frac{p_1}{\rho_1} + \frac{\rho_0^2}{\rho_1^2}\frac{c^2}{2} $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{A3}{Combining the results obtained in steps A1 and A2, express the pressure ratio $p_1/p_0$ in terms of the adiabatic exponent $\gamma$ and the compaction coefficient $k=\rho_1/\rho_0$. This relationship is called the adiabatic equation for a shock wave.}{0.50}

Let us substitute the expression for the wave front speed into the relation from the previous paragraph: $$ \frac{\gamma}{\gamma - 1}\left( \frac{p_1}{\rho_1}- \frac{p_0}{\rho_0}\right) = \frac{1}{2}\left(1-\frac{\rho_0^2}{\rho_1^2} \right) \frac{p_1- p_0}{\rho_1 - \rho_0}\frac{\rho_1}{\rho_0}. $$Упростим правую часть $$ \frac{\gamma}{\gamma - 1}\left( \frac{p_1}{\rho_1}- \frac{p_0}{\rho_0}\right) = \frac{\rho_1 + \rho_0}{2\rho_0 \rho_1} (p_1 - p_0) , $$сгруппируем terms с $p_0$ and с $p_1$: $$ p_1\left(\frac{\gamma}{\gamma - 1} \frac{1}{\rho_1} -\frac{\rho_1 + \rho_0}{2 \rho_0 \rho_1} \right) = p_0\left(\frac{\gamma}{\gamma - 1} \frac{1}{\rho_0} - \frac{\rho_1 + \rho_0}{2 \rho_0 \rho_1} \right), $$hence требуемое ratio $$ \frac{p_1}{p_0}= \frac{\frac{\gamma}{\gamma - 1} \rho_1 - \frac{1}{2}(\rho_0 + \rho_1)}{\frac{\gamma}{\gamma - 1} \rho_0 - \frac{1}{2}(\rho_0 + \rho_1)} = \frac{2 \gamma k - (\gamma -1) (1 + k)}{2 \gamma - (\gamma - 1)(1 + k) }. $$

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$ \frac{p_1}{p_0} = \frac{(\gamma + 1)k  - (\gamma -1)}{\gamma + 1 - (\gamma - 1)k }. $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{A4}{Determine the maximum possible value of the compaction coefficient $k_{max}$. Express your answer using $\gamma$. Calculate the answer for an ideal diatomic gas.}{0.50}

From the formula for the previous paragraph we see that the pressure $p_1$ goes to infinity at $k = \dfrac{\gamma + 1}{\gamma -1}$. This is the maximum value $k$. For diatomic gas $\gamma = 7/5$, $k_{max} = 6$.

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$ k_{max} = \frac{\gamma + 1}{\gamma -1} = 6. $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{B1}{Write down the law of conservation of energy for a detonation wave. Obtain from it an equation relating the parameters $p_0$, $\rho_0$, $p_1$, $\rho_1$, the speed of movement of the wave front $c$, the specific thermal effect of the reaction $q$, as well as the adiabatic exponents $\gamma_0$ and $\gamma_1$.}{0.60}

The law of conservation of energy is similar to point $\mathrm{B3}$, but you need to add the released heat $q dm$ to the work of pressure forces and take into account that the molar heat capacities of the original gas and reaction products differ: $$ p_0 \frac{dm}{\rho_0} -p_1 \frac{dm}{\rho_1} + q dm = \left( \frac{u^2}{2}  -\frac{c^2}{2}\right) dm + C_{V1} \frac{dm}{R}  \frac{p_1}{\rho_1} - C_{V0} \frac{dm}{R} \frac{p_0}{\rho_0}. $$Let us express теплоемкости gasов via/through показатели адиабаты, velocity gasа $u$ through the wave front velocity and rearrange the terms similarly to part A:

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$ \frac{\gamma_0}{\gamma_0 - 1} \frac{p_0}{\rho_0} + \frac{c^2}{2} + q = \frac{\gamma_1}{\gamma_1 -1} \frac{p_1}{\rho_1} + \frac{c^2}{2} \frac{\rho_0^2}{\rho_1^2}. $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{B2}{The explicit dependence $p_1(v_1)_\text{дет}$ for a strong detonation wave can be represented as: $$p_1=\cfrac{q}{v_0}\cdot A $$Determine $A$. Answer выtimesandте via/through $\gamma_1$ and ratio $v_1/v_0$. This relationship is called the detonation adiabatic equation.}{0.60}

In the strong wave approximation, the wavefront speed is $$ c^2 = \frac{p_1}{\rho_1 - \rho_0} \frac{\rho_1}{\rho_0} = p_1 \frac{v_0^2}{v_0 - v_1}. $$Substituting его в energy conservation law, we neglect at the same time terms $\frac{p_0}{\rho_0}$: $$ q + p_1 \frac{v_0^2}{2(v_0 - v_1)} = \frac{\gamma_1}{\gamma_1 -1}p_1 v_1 + \frac{v_1^2 }{v_0^2} p_1 \frac{v_0^2}{2(v_0 - v_1)} . $$Hence $$ p_1 \left( \frac{\gamma_1}{\gamma_1 -1} v_1  + \frac{v_1^2 - v_0^2}{2 (v_0 - v_1)}\right) = q, $$$$ p_1 = \frac{q}{\frac{\gamma_1}{\gamma_1 -1} v_1  -  \frac{1}{2} (v_0 + v_1)} = \frac{q}{v_0} \frac{2}{\frac{\gamma_1 + 1}{\gamma_1 -1} \frac{v_1}{v_0} - 1} $$

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$ A =  \frac{2}{\frac{\gamma_1 + 1}{\gamma_1 -1} \frac{v_1}{v_0} - 1}. $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{B4}{Determine the parameters $p_1$ and $v_1$ of the Chapman-Jouguet point. Express your answer using $v_0$, $\gamma_1$, $q$.}{1.40}

Let us equate the expressions for pressure through the wave speed and from the shock adiabatic equation. $$ \frac{c^2}{v_0} \left( 1 - \frac{v_1}{v_0}\right)= \frac{2q}{v_0} \frac{1}{\frac{\gamma_1 +1}{\gamma_1 - 1} \frac{v_1}{v_0} - 1}, $$we get квадратное equation на ratio $v_1/v_0$: $$ \left(1- \frac{v_1}{v_0} \right)\left( \frac{\gamma_1 +1}{\gamma_1 - 1} \frac{v_1}{v_0} - 1\right) = \frac{2q}{c^2}. $$Это equation будет иметь единственное solution, if правая часть equals максимальному значению квадратичной функции в левой части. Это максимальное value достигается for/at $$ \frac{v_1}{v_0} = \frac{\gamma_1}{\gamma_1 + 1}, $$соответствующее value equals $$ \frac{2q}{c^2} = \frac{1}{\gamma_1^2 -1}, $$hence velocity волны $$ c = \sqrt{2q (\gamma_1^2 -1)} $$and pressure $$ p_1 = \frac{c^2}{v_0}\left( 1 - \frac{v_1}{v_0}\right) = \frac{2q (\gamma_1^2-1)}{v_0} \frac{1}{\gamma_1 + 1} = \frac{2q}{v_0} (\gamma_1 -1). $$


Second solution. The equation will have a unique solution if the dependence curves $p_1(v_1)$ touch each other. Let us equate the expressions for pressures and their derivatives with respect to $v_1$:


\textbackslash{}begin{align*} \&\textbackslash{}frac{c^2}{v_0} \textbackslash{}left( 1 - \textbackslash{}frac{v_1}{v_0}\textbackslash{}right)= \textbackslash{}frac{2q}{v_0} \textbackslash{}frac{1}{\textbackslash{}frac{\textbackslash{}gamma_1 +1}{\textbackslash{}gamma_1 - 1} \textbackslash{}frac{v_1}{v_0} - 1},\textbackslash{}\textbackslash{} \&\textbackslash{}frac{c^2}{v_0^2} = \textbackslash{}frac{2q}{v_0^2} \textbackslash{}frac{\textbackslash{}gamma_1 +1}{\textbackslash{}gamma_1 -1}\textbackslash{}frac{1}{\textbackslash{}left(\textbackslash{}frac{\textbackslash{}gamma_1 +1}{\textbackslash{}gamma_1 - 1} \textbackslash{}frac{v_1}{v_0} - 1\textbackslash{}right)^2}. \textbackslash{}end{align*} Let's express the wave speed from the second equation and substitute it into the first: $$ c^2  = 2q \frac{\gamma_1 +1}{\gamma_1 -1}\frac{1}{\left(\frac{\gamma_1 +1}{\gamma_1 - 1} \frac{v_1}{v_0} - 1\right)^2}, $$$$ \frac{\gamma_1 +1}{\gamma_1 -1}\frac{1 - \frac{v_1}{v_0}}{\left(\frac{\gamma_1 +1}{\gamma_1 - 1} \frac{v_1}{v_0} - 1\right)^2} = \frac{1}{\frac{\gamma_1 +1}{\gamma_1 - 1} \frac{v_1}{v_0} - 1}, $$hence we get equation на ratio $v_1/ v_0$: $$ \frac{\gamma_1 +1}{\gamma_1 -1}\left(1 - \frac{v_1}{v_0} \right) = \frac{\gamma_1 +1}{\gamma_1 - 1} \frac{v_1}{v_0} - 1, $$from которого we find $$ v_1 = v_0 \frac{\gamma_1}{\gamma_1 + 1}. $$Substituting это value в expression for $c^2$, we get answerы for $c$ and then $p_1$.

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$ v_1 = \frac{\gamma_1}{\gamma_1 + 1} v_0, \quad p_1 =\frac{2q}{v_0} (\gamma_1 -1). $$
\end{solcard}
