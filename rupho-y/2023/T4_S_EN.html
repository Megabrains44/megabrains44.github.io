A1Write down a system of laws of conservation of mass and momentum for the volume of gas passing through the wave front of the shock wave. Determine from it the speed of movement of the wave front $c$.1.00

Let us move to a reference frame that moves with the velocity of the shock wave front $c$. In this CO, a gas with pressure $p_0$ and density $\rho_0$ impinges on a stationary front, and after the front moves with a certain speed $u$. Throughout the problem we will use this reference system. In time $dt$, mass $\rho_0 c dt$ passes through a unit area of the flow, and mass $\rho_1 u dt$ exits, therefore the law of conservation of mass $$ \rho_0 c = \rho_1 u. $$Momentum one can obtain, multiply by mass on correspond velocity. At the same time momentum gas vary for passage wave front over счет difference pressure on quantity $(p_0 - p_1) dt$, therefore $$ (\rho_1 u^2 - \rho_0 c^2 )dt = (p_0 - p_1 )dt, $$and finally $$ p_0 + \rho_0 c^2 = p_1 + \rho_1 u^2. $$Eliminate velocity $u$ with means law conservation mass: $$ u = c \frac{\rho_0}{\rho_1} $$and obtain $$ p_0 + \rho_0 c^2 = p_1 + \frac{\rho_0^2}{\rho_1} c^2, $$from which velocity wave $$ c = \sqrt{\frac{p_1 - p_0}{\rho_1 - \rho_0} \frac{\rho_1}{\rho_0}}. $$

Answer: $$ \rho_0 c = \rho_1 u, \quad p_0 + \rho_0 c^2 = p_1 + \frac{\rho_0^2}{\rho_1} c^2, \quad c = \sqrt{\frac{p_1 - p_0}{\rho_1 - \rho_0} \frac{\rho_1}{\rho_0}}. $$
A2Write down the law of conservation of energy for an element of the volume of gas passing through the wave front. Obtain from it an additional equation relating the parameters $p_0$, $\rho_0$, $p_1$, $\rho_1$, the speed of movement of the wave front $c$ and the adiabatic exponent $\gamma$.1.00

Let us consider the mass $dm$ of gas crossing the boundary. Its kinetic energy changes by $$ \Delta E_{\text{to}} = \left( \frac{u^2}{2} -\frac{c^2}{2}\right) dm, $$internal energy on $$ \Delta U = C_V \frac{dm}{\mu} (T_1 - T_0 )= C_V \frac{dm}{R} \left( \frac{p_1}{\rho_1} - \frac{p_0}{\rho_0}\right). $$Here $C_V$ – молярная heat capacity gas for constant volume, and/but temperature express through pressure with means equation state ideal gas. Above gas perform work $$ A =p_0 \frac{dm}{\rho_0} -p_1 \frac{dm}{\rho_1}. $$Therefore energy conservation law \begin{align*} A &= \Delta E_{\text{to}} + \Delta U, \\ p_0 \frac{dm}{\rho_0} -p_1 \frac{dm}{\rho_1}& = \left( \frac{u^2}{2} -\frac{c^2}{2}\right) dm + C_V \frac{dm}{R} \left( \frac{p_1}{\rho_1} - \frac{p_0}{\rho_0}\right). \end{align*} Сократим on mass and перенесем still term, относящиеся to газу to passage wave front in one side: $$ \frac{p_0}{\rho_0} + \frac{C_V}{R} \frac{p_0}{\rho_0} + \frac{c^2}{2} = \frac{p_1}{\rho_1} + \frac{C_V}{R} \frac{p_1}{\rho_1} + \frac{u^2}{2}. $$This relation – equation Бернулли, in which internal energy gas express through pressure and density. Note, that $$ \frac{C_V}{R} + 1 = \frac{C_P}{R} = \frac{C_P}{C_P-C_V} = \frac{\gamma}{\gamma - 1}, $$and/but also substitute expression for velocity $u$ through velocity wave front $c$ and we get

Answer: $$ \frac{\gamma}{\gamma - 1} \frac{p_0}{\rho_0} + \frac{c^2}{2} = \frac{\gamma}{\gamma - 1} \frac{p_1}{\rho_1} + \frac{\rho_0^2}{\rho_1^2}\frac{c^2}{2} $$
A3Combining the results obtained in steps A1 and A2, express the pressure ratio $p_1/p_0$ in terms of the adiabatic exponent $\gamma$ and the compaction coefficient $k=\rho_1/\rho_0$. This relationship is called the adiabatic equation for a shock wave.0.50

Let us substitute the expression for the wave front speed into the relation from the previous paragraph: $$ \frac{\gamma}{\gamma - 1}\left( \frac{p_1}{\rho_1}- \frac{p_0}{\rho_0}\right) = \frac{1}{2}\left(1-\frac{\rho_0^2}{\rho_1^2} \right) \frac{p_1- p_0}{\rho_1 - \rho_0}\frac{\rho_1}{\rho_0}. $$Simplify right part $$ \frac{\gamma}{\gamma - 1}\left( \frac{p_1}{\rho_1}- \frac{p_0}{\rho_0}\right) = \frac{\rho_1 + \rho_0}{2\rho_0 \rho_1} (p_1 - p_0) , $$сгруппируем term with $p_0$ and with $p_1$: $$ p_1\left(\frac{\gamma}{\gamma - 1} \frac{1}{\rho_1} -\frac{\rho_1 + \rho_0}{2 \rho_0 \rho_1} \right) = p_0\left(\frac{\gamma}{\gamma - 1} \frac{1}{\rho_0} - \frac{\rho_1 + \rho_0}{2 \rho_0 \rho_1} \right), $$from which требуемое ratio $$ \frac{p_1}{p_0}= \frac{\frac{\gamma}{\gamma - 1} \rho_1 - \frac{1}{2}(\rho_0 + \rho_1)}{\frac{\gamma}{\gamma - 1} \rho_0 - \frac{1}{2}(\rho_0 + \rho_1)} = \frac{2 \gamma k - (\gamma -1) (1 + k)}{2 \gamma - (\gamma - 1)(1 + k) }. $$

Answer: $$ \frac{p_1}{p_0} = \frac{(\gamma + 1)k - (\gamma -1)}{\gamma + 1 - (\gamma - 1)k }. $$
A4Determine the maximum possible value of the compaction coefficient $k_{max}$. Express your answer using $\gamma$. Calculate the answer for an ideal diatomic gas.0.50

From the formula for the previous paragraph we see that the pressure $p_1$ goes to infinity at $k = \dfrac{\gamma + 1}{\gamma -1}$. This is the maximum value $k$. For diatomic gas $\gamma = 7/5$, $k_{max} = 6$.

Answer: $$ k_{max} = \frac{\gamma + 1}{\gamma -1} = 6. $$

B2The explicit dependence $p_1(v_1)_\text{det}$ for a strong detonation wave can be represented as: $$p_1=\cfrac{q}{v_0}\cdot A $$Determine $A$. Answer express through $\gamma_1$ and ratio $v_1/v_0$. This relationship is called the detonation adiabatic equation.0.60

In the strong wave approximation, the wavefront speed is $$ c^2 = \frac{p_1}{\rho_1 - \rho_0} \frac{\rho_1}{\rho_0} = p_1 \frac{v_0^2}{v_0 - v_1}. $$Substitute it in energy conservation law, neglect at the same time term $\frac{p_0}{\rho_0}$: $$ q + p_1 \frac{v_0^2}{2(v_0 - v_1)} = \frac{\gamma_1}{\gamma_1 -1}p_1 v_1 + \frac{v_1^2 }{v_0^2} p_1 \frac{v_0^2}{2(v_0 - v_1)} . $$Hence $$ p_1 \left( \frac{\gamma_1}{\gamma_1 -1} v_1 + \frac{v_1^2 - v_0^2}{2 (v_0 - v_1)}\right) = q, $$$$ p_1 = \frac{q}{\frac{\gamma_1}{\gamma_1 -1} v_1 - \frac{1}{2} (v_0 + v_1)} = \frac{q}{v_0} \frac{2}{\frac{\gamma_1 + 1}{\gamma_1 -1} \frac{v_1}{v_0} - 1} $$

Answer: $$ A = \frac{2}{\frac{\gamma_1 + 1}{\gamma_1 -1} \frac{v_1}{v_0} - 1}. $$

B4Determine the parameters $p_1$ and $v_1$ of the Chapman-Jouguet point. Express your answer using $v_0$, $\gamma_1$, $q$.1.40

Let us equate the expressions for pressure through the wave speed and from the shock adiabatic equation. $$ \frac{c^2}{v_0} \left( 1 - \frac{v_1}{v_0}\right)= \frac{2q}{v_0} \frac{1}{\frac{\gamma_1 +1}{\gamma_1 - 1} \frac{v_1}{v_0} - 1}, $$obtain square equation on ratio $v_1/v_0$: $$ \left(1- \frac{v_1}{v_0} \right)\left( \frac{\gamma_1 +1}{\gamma_1 - 1} \frac{v_1}{v_0} - 1\right) = \frac{2q}{c^2}. $$This equation be have unique solution, if right part equal maximum value quadratic function in left part. This maximum value be achieved for $$ \frac{v_1}{v_0} = \frac{\gamma_1}{\gamma_1 + 1}, $$correspond value equal $$ \frac{2q}{c^2} = \frac{1}{\gamma_1^2 -1}, $$from which velocity wave $$ c = \sqrt{2q (\gamma_1^2 -1)} $$and pressure $$ p_1 = \frac{c^2}{v_0}\left( 1 - \frac{v_1}{v_0}\right) = \frac{2q (\gamma_1^2-1)}{v_0} \frac{1}{\gamma_1 + 1} = \frac{2q}{v_0} (\gamma_1 -1). $$

Second solution. The equation will have a unique solution if the dependence curves $p_1(v_1)$ touch each other. Let us equate the expressions for pressures and their derivatives with respect to $v_1$:

\begin{align*} &\frac{c^2}{v_0} \left( 1 - \frac{v_1}{v_0}\right)= \frac{2q}{v_0} \frac{1}{\frac{\gamma_1 +1}{\gamma_1 - 1} \frac{v_1}{v_0} - 1},\\ &\frac{c^2}{v_0^2} = \frac{2q}{v_0^2} \frac{\gamma_1 +1}{\gamma_1 -1}\frac{1}{\left(\frac{\gamma_1 +1}{\gamma_1 - 1} \frac{v_1}{v_0} - 1\right)^2}. \end{align*} Let's express the wave speed from the second equation and substitute it into the first: $$ c^2 = 2q \frac{\gamma_1 +1}{\gamma_1 -1}\frac{1}{\left(\frac{\gamma_1 +1}{\gamma_1 - 1} \frac{v_1}{v_0} - 1\right)^2}, $$$$ \frac{\gamma_1 +1}{\gamma_1 -1}\frac{1 - \frac{v_1}{v_0}}{\left(\frac{\gamma_1 +1}{\gamma_1 - 1} \frac{v_1}{v_0} - 1\right)^2} = \frac{1}{\frac{\gamma_1 +1}{\gamma_1 - 1} \frac{v_1}{v_0} - 1}, $$hence obtain equation on ratio $v_1/ v_0$: $$ \frac{\gamma_1 +1}{\gamma_1 -1}\left(1 - \frac{v_1}{v_0} \right) = \frac{\gamma_1 +1}{\gamma_1 - 1} \frac{v_1}{v_0} - 1, $$from which find $$ v_1 = v_0 \frac{\gamma_1}{\gamma_1 + 1}. $$Substitute this value in expression for $c^2$, obtain answer for $c$ and then $p_1$.

Answer: $$ v_1 = \frac{\gamma_1}{\gamma_1 + 1} v_0, \quad p_1 =\frac{2q}{v_0} (\gamma_1 -1). $$
