Adiabatic invariant and magnetic mirrors

Y21 (2021) — English translation

Let us consider a classical mechanical system with some parameter $\lambda$, which can slowly change over time. If the motion of the system is periodic with a period $T$, the change in the parameter can be considered slow provided $$ T\frac{d\lambda}{dt}\ll{\lambda}. $$ В общем случае по временем $T$ можно понимать характерное время процессов, протекающих в системе. Адиабатический инвариант – физическая величина, которая не меняется при плавном изменении параметров системы. Можно доказать, что адиабатическим инвариантом является величина $$ I=\frac{1}{2\pi}\oint pdq. $$ Здесь $p$ – одна из проекций импульса системы, $q$ – соответствующая координата. Если изобразить периодическое движение частицы в координатах $p$, $q$, при периодическом движении получим замкнутую кривую. Тогда интеграл в определении $I$ is equal to the area bounded by this curve.

Part A. Movement between two walls (0.8 points)

Fig. 1.

A bead of mass $m$ can slide along a smooth horizontal spoke between two vertical walls. All impacts of the bead with the walls are absolutely elastic. One wall is stationary, and the second wall moves towards the first with a speed $u$, satisfying the relation $u\ll{v}$, where $v$ is the speed of the bead. The initial distance between the walls is $L_0$, the initial speed of the bead is $v_0$.

A1
Find the speed of the bead immediately after colliding with the moving wall.
0.20
A2
Find the change in the distance between the walls during the time between two successive collisions of the bead with the moving wall. It is necessary to obtain the exact formula.
0.20
A3
Find an expression for the adiabatic invariant $I$ for the considered system and determine the speed of the bead at the moment when the distance between the walls has decreased to the value $L$.
0.40

Part E. Mathematical pendulum on an inclined plane (1.3 points)

A mathematical pendulum is attached to a smooth inclined plane, making small oscillations near a stable equilibrium position. In this part of the problem, you are asked to investigate how the amplitude of oscillations of a pendulum changes with a change in the angle of inclination of the plane, as well as the length of the pendulum. At the initial moment, the amplitude of oscillations (the angle of deviation of the pendulum from the equilibrium position is measured) is equal to $\varphi_0 \ll 1$, the length of the pendulum is equal to $L_0$, and the angle of inclination of the plane is $\alpha_0$.

E1
Find the adiabatic invariant $I$ for a harmonic oscillator with mass $m$ oscillating with cyclic frequency $\omega_0$ and amplitude $A$.
0.50
E2
Let the length of the pendulum be adiabatically slowly increased to $L$ at a constant angle of inclination. Find the new amplitude of the pendulum $\varphi$. Express your answer in terms of $\varphi_0, \alpha_0, L_0$ and $L$.
0.40
E3
Let the pendulum's length remain constant and the angle of inclination be adiabatically slowly increased to the value $\alpha$. Find the new vibration amplitude $\varphi$. Express your answer in terms of $\varphi_0, \alpha_0, \alpha$ and $L_0$.
0.40

Part G. Motion in a non-uniform magnetic field (3.0 points)

One method of keeping particles in a magnetic trap in the longitudinal (along the field) direction is to use magnetic plugs, or magnetic mirrors, areas in which the magnetic field strength increases strongly (but smoothly). Such areas can reflect charged particles incident on them along the field lines. When a charged particle moves in a “mirror” magnetic trap, the radius of the particle’s trajectory decreases as it moves into the region of a strong field. In the last two parts we will study movement in such fields. For magnetic mirrors, we define the specularity coefficient $r_m$ $$r_m=\frac{B_{max}}{B_{min}} $$ Во всех пунктах считайте, что траектории частиц представляют собой медленно изменяющиеся спирали с малым углом наклона, ведущие центры которых совпадают с силовыми линиями магнитного поля. Введём также компоненты скорости $v_{||}$ и $v_\perp$ ($v_{||}\ll v_{\perp}$), denoting the particle velocity components directed along and perpendicular to the direction of the magnetic field, respectively.

G1
Let us first consider plane motion in a uniform magnetic field with induction $B_0$. Let its time derivative be equal to $\dot{B}$ and the change occurs adiabatically slowly. The initial velocity of the particle is $v_0$, its mass is $M$, and its charge is $q$. Express the tangential acceleration of particle $\dot{{v_\perp}}$ in terms of $M, q, v_0, B_0$ and $\dot{B}$.
0.20
G2
Let the magnetic field slowly change its value to the value $B$. Express the speed of particle $v_\perp$ in terms of $v_0, B_0$ and $B$.
0.50

Note: We have already mentioned that any charged particles have a magnetic moment. Therefore, you just need to consider a circuit with direct current moving in a non-uniform magnetic field.

Fig. 5.

Currents of equal strength $I$ flow through the turns in one direction. the turns are placed in a region of a large uniform magnetic field with induction $B_0$, directed along their axes.
