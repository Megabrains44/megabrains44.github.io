Acceleration due to gravity depends not just on the mass $m$, but on $m\cdot{\mu\left(\frac{a}{a_0}\right)}$, where $\mu(x)$ is a certain function, the value of which tends to unity for large arguments ($x\gg{1}$), and to $x$ for small arguments ( $x$ is slightly less than $1$), $a$ - the acceleration due to gravity, $a_0$, is constant. Thus, $2$ Newton's law takes the form: $F=m\cdot{\mu\left(\frac{a}{a_0}\right)a}$. The centripetal acceleration of stars in gas clouds on the outskirts of spiral galaxies will, as is correct, be less than $a_0$.
