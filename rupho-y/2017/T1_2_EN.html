Steam in a test tube

Y17 (2017) — English translation

A thin test tube is partially filled with water and placed vertically in a large volume chamber in which the air is maintained at zero relative humidity and a constant pressure equal to external atmospheric pressure. Due to diffusion in the test tube, a linear change in the vapor concentration with height is established: near the surface of the water, the vapor turns out to be saturated, and at the upper open end of the test tube its concentration is $2$ times less. The top of the test tube is closed with a lid. After equilibrium was established, it turned out that the density of moist air inside the test tube differs from the density of dry air away from the test tube by $\Delta{\rho}=5.4~\text{г}/\text{м}^3$. Molar mass of dry air $\mu_\text{в}=29~\text{г}/\text{моль}$, vapor $\mu_\text{п}=18~\text{г}/\text{моль}$. Neglect the change in the liquid level in the test tube during the experiment.

A1
Find the saturated vapor pressure at the experimental temperature equal to $T=27^{\circ}\text{C}$.
