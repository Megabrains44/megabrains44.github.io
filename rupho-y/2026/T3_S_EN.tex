\begin{solcard}{A1}{Determine the magnitude of the magnetic field $B(t)$ of the electromagnets at the moment when the energy of the electrons $E(t)$. Express your answer in terms of $E(t)$, $T$ and physical constants.}{0.50}

Let's write down Newton's second law: $$e v B = p \frac{2\pi}{T}.$$$$B = \frac{2\pi}{eT}\cdot\frac{p}{v} = \frac{2\pi}{eT}\cdot\gamma m = \frac{2\pi}{eT}\cdot\frac{E}{c^2}$$

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$B(t) = \frac{2\pi E(t)}{eT c^2}$$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{A2}{Determine at what ratio there are equilibrium electrons on $T$, $U$ and $dB/dt$.}{0.40}

Let's express the energy from the result of the previous point and differentiate taking into account the fact that $T = \text{const}$. $$\frac{dE}{dt} = \frac{eTc^2}{2\pi}\cdot\frac{dB}{dt} = \frac{eU}{T}\cos\varphi$$$$\cos\varphi = \frac{T^2c^2}{2\pi U}\cdot\frac{dB}{dt}$$The cosine does not exceed 1, which provides a condition for the permissible parameters of the synchrotron.

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$\frac{T^2c^2}{2\pi U}\cdot\frac{dB}{dt} \leqslant 1$$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{A3}{Find the dependence of frequency $\omega_U(t)$ on time. Express your answer in terms of $B(t)$, $n$, $R$ and physical constants.}{0.50}

Newton's second law is similar to the previous points: $$e v B = p \frac{2\pi}{T} = \frac{p \omega_U}{n}.$$Let us express $p$ and $E$: $$p = eB\frac{vT}{2\pi} = eBR.$$$$E=\sqrt{(m_p c^2)^2 + (pc)^2}=\sqrt{(m_p c^2)^2 + (ceBR)^2}$$Relation $E$ and $B$ можно поrayandть and similarly прошлому partу: $$eB = \frac{p \omega_U}{vn} = \frac{E\omega_U}{c^2n}.$$$$\omega_U(t) = \frac{nec^2B}{E}$$All that remains is to substitute the energy value.

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$\omega_U(t) = \frac{nec^2B(t)}{\sqrt{(m_p c^2)^2 + \left(ceB(t)R\right)^2}}$$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{B1}{Obtain the value $\alpha$ for the weak focus case.}{0.50}

Similar to the previous point, the expression for momentum in a circular orbit with radius $R$: $$p(R) = eB(R)R=\text{const}\cdot R^{1-\xi}.$$Then relation на относительные изменения: $$\frac{dp}{p} = (1-\xi)\frac{dR}{R} = (1-\xi)\frac{dL}{L}.$$

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$\alpha = \frac{1}{1-\xi}$$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{B2}{Express $K$ in terms of $\gamma$ and $\alpha$.}{1.50}

Let's use the expression for the cyclic frequency: $$\omega = \frac{2\pi v}{L}.$$$$\frac{d\omega}{\omega} = \frac{dv}{v} - \frac{dL}{L} = \frac{dv}{v} - \alpha\frac{dp}{p}$$$$K = \frac{E}{v}\frac{dv}{dE} - \alpha\frac{E}{p}\frac{dp}{dE}$$Let us find производные and substituting. $$\frac{vdE}{Edv} = \frac{vd\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-v^2/c^2}}}{dv/\sqrt{1-v^2/c^2}} = -\frac{v}{\sqrt{1-v^2/c^2}}\cdot \frac{d\sqrt{1-v^2/c^2}}{dv} = \frac{v^2}{c^2-v^2} = \beta^2\gamma^2 = \left(1-\frac{1}{\gamma^2}\right)\gamma^2 = \gamma^2-1$$$$\frac{p}{E}\frac{dE}{dp} = \frac{p}{\sqrt{(mc)^2+p^2}}\frac{d\sqrt{(mc)^2+p^2}}{dp} = \frac{p^2}{(mc)^2+p^2} = 1-\frac{(mc^2)^2}{E^2}=1-\frac{1}{\gamma^2}= \frac{\gamma^2-1}{\gamma^2}$$

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$K = \frac{1-\alpha\gamma^2}{\gamma^2-1}$$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{B3}{Note at what focusing the critical energy exists. Find $\gamma$ for particles with critical energy.}{0.50}

For a critical situation we have: $$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\alpha}}.$$$\gamma > 1$, therefore for the existence of critical energy it is necessary $\alpha < 1$. For weak focusing this condition is not met.

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} Critical energy only exists for strong focus. $$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\alpha}}$$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{C1}{Considering that the initial energies of the particles differ only slightly, express $\dot \varphi$ in terms of $K$, $\mathcal E$, $n$, $\omega_0$ and $E_0$.}{0.90}

Let us consider the change in the phase of a particle in contrast to the equilibrium one during one revolution: $$\Delta \varphi =\omega_U\left(\frac{2\pi}{\omega} - \frac{2\pi}{\omega_0}\right).$$Dividing на time этого изменения, that is period обращения частицы equalй $2\pi/\omega$. $$\dot\varphi = \omega_U\left(1-\frac{\omega}{\omega_0}\right) = n(\omega_0 - \omega)$$Let us use оlimitением $K$ and we get: $$\omega - \omega_0 = K\frac{\omega_0}{E_0}\mathcal E.$$

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$\dot\varphi = -\frac{nK\omega_0}{E_0}\mathcal E$$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{C2}{Express $\dot{\mathcal{E}}$ in terms of $\varphi_0$, $\varphi$, $U$, $\omega_0$ and physical constants. Use the fact that the difference between $\omega$ and $\omega_0$ is small enough to be neglected in this case.}{0.30}

Let us write down the derivatives $E$ and $E_0$. $$\dot E = \frac{\omega}{2\pi} eU \cos\varphi \approx \frac{\omega_0}{2\pi} eU \cos\varphi$$$$\dot E_0 =  \frac{\omega_0}{2\pi} eU \cos\varphi_0$$

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$\dot{\mathcal E} = \frac{\omega_0eU}{2\pi}\left(\cos\varphi - \cos\varphi_0\right)$$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{С3}{Obtain an expression for $\ddot\varphi$ in terms of $K$, $E_0$, $\omega_0$, $U$, $\varphi_0$, $\varphi$, $n$ and physical constants. Determine at what $\varphi_0$ the equilibrium orbit is stable (that is, the initially infinitesimal deviations $\varphi$ do not grow with time).}{0.60}

Let us differentiate $\dot \varphi$ taking into account the indicated approximations. $$\ddot\varphi=-\frac{nK\omega_0}{E_0}\dot{\mathcal E}$$


For stability, we expand to first order: $$\ddot\varphi=\frac{nK\omega^2_0eU}{2\pi E_0}\sin\varphi_0 (\varphi - \varphi_0).$$

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$\ddot\varphi=-\frac{nK\omega^2_0eU}{2\pi E_0}\left(\cos\varphi - \cos\varphi_0\right)$$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{C4}{Express $\dot \varphi^2$ in terms of $K$, $E_0$, $\omega_0$, $U$, $\varphi_0$, $\varphi$, $n$, $\varphi_i$ and $\mathcal{E}_i$ and physical constants.}{0.50}

Let's use the result of the previous paragraph. $$\ddot\varphi = \frac{d\dot\varphi}{dt} = \frac{d\dot\varphi}{d\varphi}\frac{d\varphi}{dt}  =\frac{d\dot\varphi^2}{2d\varphi}$$$$d\dot\varphi^2 = -\frac{nK\omega^2_0eU}{\pi E_0}\left(\cos\varphi - \cos\varphi_0\right)d\varphi$$$$\dot\varphi^2 + \frac{nK\omega^2_0eU}{\pi E_0}\left(\sin\varphi - \varphi\cos\varphi_0\right) = \text{const}$$Осталось подставить начальные значения. $$\dot\varphi_i = -\frac{nK\omega_0}{E_0}\mathcal E_i$$

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$\dot\varphi^2 = \left(\frac{nK\omega_0}{E_0}\mathcal E_i\right)^2+\frac{nK\omega^2_0eU}{\pi E_0}\left(\sin\varphi_i - \varphi_i\cos\varphi_0\right) - \frac{nK\omega^2_0eU}{\pi E_0}\left(\sin\varphi - \varphi\cos\varphi_0\right) $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{С5}{Determine at what values ​​of $\varphi_i$ and $\mathcal E_i$ the particle is involved in the acceleration process, that is, on average it receives the same amount of energy as the equilibrium one. Qualitatively depict the boundaries of the found region of the $(\varphi_i, \mathcal E_i)$ plane for $\varphi_0=0$ (resonant acceleration) and $\varphi=\pi/4$.}{1.80}

Let's use the result of the previous point: $$\dot\varphi^2 + \frac{nK\omega^2_0eU}{\pi E_0}\left(\sin\varphi - \varphi\cos\varphi_0\right) = \text{const} = \left(\frac{nK\omega_0}{E_0}\mathcal E_i\right)^2+\frac{nK\omega^2_0eU}{\pi E_0}\left(\sin\varphi_i - \varphi_i\cos\varphi_0\right).$$Note that вовлечение в acceleration equalsсильно ограниченности движения $\varphi$. $$\dot\varphi^2 + V(\varphi) = \text{const} = H$$Поrayandли задачу исследования движения в некотором "potentialе" $V$ с "энергией" $H$. Consider сrayай $K>0$. Построим качественный вид $V$ for this case.


Red indicates the maximum level of “energy” at which the movement is finite (occurs in a limited area). With greater “energy” $\varphi$ can grow unlimitedly (movement to the right on the graph). Let's find this value $H$. It is determined by the value of the maximum $V$. $$\frac{dV}{d\varphi} = \frac{nK\omega^2_0eU}{\pi E_0}\left(\cos\varphi-\cos\varphi_0\right)$$Максимум соответствует $\varphi = |\varphi_0|$. $$H = \frac{nK\omega^2_0eU}{\pi E_0}\left(\sin|\varphi _0|- |\varphi_0|\cos\varphi_0\right)$$Then condition на $\varphi_i$ and $\mathcal E_i$ имеет вид: $$\left(\frac{nK\omega_0}{E_0}\mathcal E_i\right)^2+\frac{nK\omega^2_0eU}{\pi E_0}\left(\sin\varphi_i - \varphi_i\cos\varphi_0\right) \leqslant \frac{nK\omega^2_0eU}{\pi E_0}\left(\sin|\varphi _0|- |\varphi_0|\cos\varphi_0\right).$$Стоит отметить, что at the same time существует дополнительное condition на $\varphi_i$. Fromначально частица должна находится в "potentialьной яме", что соответствует $\varphi_i \leqslant |\varphi_0|$. Note that for/at $K<0$ ситуация аналогичная, но теперь potential имеет максимум for/at $\varphi = -|\varphi_0|$. Then максимальная "energy": $$H = \frac{nK\omega^2_0eU}{\pi E_0}\left(-\sin|\varphi _0|+ |\varphi_0|\cos\varphi_0\right).$$И similarlyе ограничение на фазу $\varphi_i \geqslant -|\varphi_0|$. Note that можно обобщить на произвольный sign $K$: $$H = \frac{n|K|\omega^2_0eU}{\pi E_0}\left(\sin|\varphi _0|- |\varphi_0|\cos\varphi_0\right).$$$$\frac{K}{|K|} \varphi_i \leqslant|\varphi_0|$$Final expression for the desired area:


Region border: $$\left(\frac{nK\omega_0}{E_0}\mathcal E_i\right)^2+\frac{nK\omega^2_0eU}{\pi E_0}\left(\sin\varphi_i - \varphi_i\cos\varphi_0\right) = \frac{n|K|\omega^2_0eU}{\pi E_0}\left(\sin|\varphi _0|- |\varphi_0|\cos\varphi_0\right);$$$$\frac{K}{|K|} \varphi_i \leqslant|\varphi_0|.$$


Case $\varphi_0 = 0$


$$\left(\frac{nK\omega_0}{E_0}\mathcal E_i\right)^2+\frac{nK\omega^2_0eU}{\pi E_0}\left(\sin\varphi_i - \varphi_i\right) = 0;$$$$K \varphi_i \leqslant 0.$$Несложно заметить, что for/at $\varphi_i \neq 0$: $$K\left(\sin\varphi_i - \varphi_i\right) = -K\varphi_i\left(1-\frac{\sin\varphi_i}{\varphi_i}\right).$$At the same time $1-\frac{\sin\varphi_i}{\varphi_i}>0$, therefore: $$K\left(\sin\varphi_i - \varphi_i\right) = -K\varphi_i\left(1-\frac{\sin\varphi_i}{\varphi_i}\right) > 0.$$Substituting в первое неequality and we get: $$\left(\frac{nK\omega_0}{E_0}\mathcal E_i\right)^2 < 0.$$Thus, единственная точка, удовлетворяющая условиям $(\varphi_i,\mathcal E_i) = (0,0)$. Стоить отметить, что данный факт очевиден from рассмотрения энергетической аналогии. For/At $\varphi_0 = 0$ potential has no minimum, there is only an inflection point at zero, so the only case of limited movement is equilibrium at zero.


Case $\varphi_0 = \pi/4$


$$\left(\frac{nK\omega_0}{E_0}\mathcal E_i\right)^2+\frac{nK\omega^2_0eU}{\pi E_0}\left(\sin\varphi_i - \frac{\varphi_i}{\sqrt 2}\right) = \frac{n|K|\omega^2_0eU}{\pi E_0}\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt 2}- \frac{\pi}{4\sqrt 2}\right)$$$$\frac{K}{|K|} \varphi_i \leqslant\frac{\pi}{4}$$Поrayandли два сrayая в зависимости от signа $K$:

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$\left(\frac{nK\omega_0}{E_0}\mathcal E_i\right)^2+\frac{nK\omega^2_0eU}{\pi E_0}\left(\sin\varphi_i - \varphi_i\cos\varphi_0\right) \leqslant \frac{n|K|\omega^2_0eU}{\pi E_0}\left(\sin|\varphi _0|- |\varphi_0|\cos\varphi_0\right);$$$$\frac{K}{|K|} \varphi_i \leqslant|\varphi_0|.$$
