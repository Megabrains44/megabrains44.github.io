\maketitle

The Carnot heat engine operates with two heat reservoirs of finite heat capacity $C=500~\text{J/K}$, which at the initial moment of time are at temperatures $T_h=625~\text{K}$ and $T_c=298~\text{K}$. The power developed by a heat engine decreases exponentially with time $P=P_0\,\mathrm{e}^{-kt}$, where $P_0=960~\text{W}$ is the power at the initial moment of time, and $t$ is the time elapsed since the initial moment.

\problembox{A1}{At what temperature of the thermal reservoirs will the heat engine stop working?}{1.00}

\problembox{A2}{How much work will the heat engine do during its operation?}{1.00}

\problembox{A3}{After what time will the power developed by the heat engine be halved?}{1.00}

\problembox{A4}{After what time will the efficiency of a heat engine decrease by half?}{1.00}

\problembox{A5}{How long will it take for the temperature difference between the heat reservoirs to be halved?}{1.00}
