Hydrodynamic focusing

X21 (2021) — English translation

Some applications require extremely narrow fluid flows. The narrowness of the flow significantly accelerates the mixing of the substance with the environment, which makes it possible to study fast chemical reactions. Also, the use of narrow flows allows you to reduce the amount of substance used. If you create a narrow flow using a piston that forces fluid through a narrow opening, the resulting flow is extremely turbulent. This makes experiments very difficult. Making fairly narrow channels is also quite labor-intensive. These difficulties can be circumvented by compressing the flow by surrounding it with a flow of another fluid. Depending on the flow parameters, the width of the central part of the flow can significantly decrease. This phenomenon is called hydrodynamic focusing. In this problem we will consider the simplest models of hydrodynamic focusing.

It is known that if the fluid velocity depends only on the coordinate $x$ and is directed along the axis $y$, then the force $$ F = \eta S \frac{dv}{dx}. $$ $S$ – площадь соприкосновения жидкостей. Сила, действующая на более медленно движущийся слой направлена в сторону его движения. Рассмотрим течение жидкости в щели между двумя параллельными плоскостями. Расстояние между двумя плоскостями $d$, ширина плоскостей $w$, длина $L$. Разность давлений на краях системы $\Delta acts between two layers of fluid P$. Вязкость жидкости $\eta$. Ось $x$ направлена перпендикулярно плоскостям, ось $y$ – в направлении течения жидкости. Мы считаем, что $d\ll w$, поэтому можно пренебрегать эффектами на границе считать, что скорость течения зависит только от $x$ и всюду направлена вдоль оси $y$. We consider the flow to be stationary everywhere, that is, the velocity distribution does not depend on time.

For both flow through a slit and flow through a tube, the flow is proportional to the pressure difference at the ends. The dependence of flow on pressure can be written in the form $q = \Delta P/ R$, where $R$ is the hydrodynamic resistance of the tube (or slot).

Part B. Two types of liquids (1.4 points)

Now let two types of liquids flow between the planes. The sample we are interested in flows in the central section (we will denote all quantities related to it with index 1). The width of its flow is $w_1$. The remaining part of the flow (at the edges) is occupied by liquid 2, which compresses the sample (see figure). In subsequent parts of the problem, we will be interested in the ratio of the sample width to the total flow width $w_1/w$. We will assume that the flow width is much larger than the slot thickness $w_1 \gg d$. Then the influence of the area of ​​contact of liquids on the flow can be neglected, and it can be assumed that the speed of the liquid at each point depends only on its properties and the $x$ coordinate (the $Ox$ axis is perpendicular to the planes). At the boundaries of the gap at $x = \pm d/2$ the velocity becomes 0 as in part A.

Liquids 1 and 2 come from two reservoirs located at heights $h_1$ and $h_2$ under the influence of gravity. These tanks are connected to the main part of the installation by a system of pipes. We can assume that the fluid flow through each tube does not depend on time and is proportional to the pressure difference at its ends $$ q_i = \Delta P_i/R_i. $$ Здесь $R_i$ is the hydrostatic resistance of the tube. This resistance depends on the parameters of the tube and the properties of the liquid. The pressure difference at the ends of the tube is created by gravity.

In order to make the flow independent of the slot parameters, it is necessary that the resistance of the tubes be much greater than the resistance of all other parts of the circuit. To do this, special very narrow cylindrical capillaries - rheostats - are added to the tubes. In what follows, we will assume that the entire resistance of the supply tubes is determined by the resistance of these rheostats. Let us assume that the geometric dimensions of the rheostats (their radii and lengths) for the sources of both liquids are the same.

Part D. Experiment (3.1 points)

Water is used as liquid 2, which is constantly supplied from height $h_2 = 0.5~м$. The density of water is $\rho_2 = 10^3~kg/м^3$. Water viscosity $\eta = 8.9 \cdot10^{-4} kg/(м \cdot c)$ The rheostat is a capillary with radius $r= 130~мкм$ and length $L=0.5~м$. A slit with length $l = 43~мм$, width $w= 3~мм$, $d = 175~ мкм$ is used.
