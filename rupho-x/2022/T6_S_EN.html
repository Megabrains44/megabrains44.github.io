Oscillations in a charged cylinder --- Solution

X22 (2022) — English translation

A1A disk with radius $R$ is charged with surface charge density $\sigma_R$. Determine the potential $\varphi(y)$ at a point on the axis at a distance $y$ from the center of the disk. The potential is zero at infinity.0.50

Potential from a ring of width $dr$ with radius $r$ $$d \varphi = k \frac{\sigma_R \cdot 2 \pi r dr}{\sqrt{y^2 + r^2} }$$ Potential from all disk find integration: $$\varphi(y)= \pi k \sigma_R \int^R_0 \frac{d(r^2)}{\sqrt{y^2 + r^2} } =2πk \sigma_R \left( \sqrt{R^2+y^2}- |y| \right)$$

Answer: $$\varphi(y)=2πk \sigma_R \left( \sqrt{R^2+y^2}- |y| \right)$$
A2Two such disks of radius $R$ charged with surface charge density $\sigma_R>0$ are parallel to each other. The distance between the centers of the disks is $2L$, the centers are located on the axis of the disks. In the equilibrium position there is a charge $q$ with mass $m$, which can only move along the axis of the disks. Determine the angular frequency $\omega_1$ of vibrations of such a charge. What is the charge sign?1.00

When the charge is displaced by $x$ along the axis, an outer ring of width $\delta = 2 Rx/L$ appears on the nearest disk, which creates a restoring force. The total field of the remaining charges is zero. The field of this ring is $$E_x(x) = - k \frac{2\pi R \delta \sigma_R}{(L^2+R^2)} \frac{L}{\sqrt{L^2+R^2}}=-\frac{\sigma_R R^2}{\varepsilon_0 (L^2+R^2)^{3/2}}x.$$ Equation of motion charge $$m\ddot{x}=qE(x).$$ Angular frequency for oscillation: $$\omega^2=\frac{q\sigma_R R^2}{m \varepsilon_0 (L^2+R^2)^{3/2}}.$$

Answer: $$\omega^2=\frac{q\sigma_R R^2}{m \varepsilon_0 (L^2+R^2)^{3/2}},$$ $$q>0.$$
A3Now this charge can only move in a perpendicular direction. Express the angular frequency $\omega_2$ of oscillations in this case in terms of $\omega_1$. What is the charge sign now?1.00

We use Gauss's theorem for a small cylinder between the disks to find the dependence of the radial field $E_r(r)$ on the displacement from the axis $r$: $$2\pi r \cdot 2x E_r(r) + 2\pi r^2 E_x(x) = 0$$ $$E_r(r)=-\frac{1}{2}E_x(x)\frac{r}{x}.$$ Charge must be other sign. It can be seen, that coefficient in linear dependence in 2 times меньше, therefore, $$\omega_2 = \frac{\omega_1}{\sqrt{2}}.$$

Answer: $$\omega_2 = \frac{\omega_1}{\sqrt{2}},$$ $$q<0.$$
B1The lateral surface of a cylinder with radius $R$ and length $L$ is charged with surface charge density $\sigma_L$. Determine the potential at a point on the axis at a distance $z$ from the center of one of the bases of the cylinder. The potential is zero at infinity.1.00

Potential from a ring of height $dl$ at a distance $l$ from zero $$d \varphi (y) = k \frac{\sigma_L \cdot 2 \pi R dl}{\sqrt{(z+l)^2 + R^2}}.$$ Potential from all боковой surface cylinder find integration: $$\varphi (y) = 2 \pi k R \sigma_L \int^{L+z}_z \frac{d (l+z)}{\sqrt{(l+z)^2 + R^2}} = 2\pi k R\sigma_L \left( \mathrm{arth} \frac{L+z}{\sqrt{(L+z)^2 + R^2}} - \mathrm{arth} \frac{z}{\sqrt{z^2 + R^2}}\right).$$

Answer: $$\varphi (y) = 2\pi k R \sigma_L \left( \mathrm{arth} \frac{L+z}{\sqrt{(L+z)^2 + R^2}} - \mathrm{arth} \frac{z}{\sqrt{z^2 + R^2}}\right).$$
B2Two such cylinders (with radius $R$ and length $L$, surface charged with surface charge density $\sigma_L>0$) are placed close together and have a common axis. In the equilibrium position there is a charge $q$ with mass $m$, which can only move along the axis of the cylinders. Determine the angular frequency $\omega_3$ of vibrations of such a charge. What is the charge sign?1.00

When the charge is displaced by $x$ along the axis, an outer ring of width $\delta = 2x$ appears on the far cylinder, which creates a restoring force. The total field of the remaining charges is zero. The field of this ring is $$E_x(x) = k \frac{2\pi R \delta \sigma_L}{(L^2+R^2)} \frac{L}{\sqrt{L^2+R^2}}=\frac{\sigma_L R L}{\varepsilon_0 (L^2+R^2)^{3/2}}x.$$ Equation of motion charge $$m\ddot{x}=qE_x(x),$$ it can be seen, that charge must be negative. Angular frequency for oscillation: $$\omega_3^2=\frac{|q|\sigma_L R L}{m \varepsilon_0 (L^2+R^2)^{3/2}}.$$

Answer: $$\omega_3^2=\frac{|q|\sigma_L R L}{m \varepsilon_0 (L^2+R^2)^{3/2}},$$ $$q<0.$$
B3Now this charge can only move in a perpendicular direction. Express the angular frequency $\omega_4$ of oscillations in this case in terms of $\omega_3$. What is the charge sign now?0.50

Everything happens similarly to A3, that is, $$E_r(r)=-\frac{1}{2}E_x(x)\frac{r}{x},$$ means $\omega^2_4 = \omega^2_3/2$ and the charge should also change sign.

Answer: $$\omega_4 = \frac{\omega_3}{\sqrt{2}},$$ $$q>0.$$
С1A charged cylinder with radius $R$ and height $L=40R/9$ consists of a side surface and one base. Surface charge density of the side surface $\sigma_L$, base $\sigma_R$. If you place a point charge at the center of the opposite base, it will be in an equilibrium position. Define the relation $\sigma_L/\sigma_R$.1.50

The field strength created by the base of the cylinder will be found as $$E_R = - \left. \frac{d \varphi}{d y} \right|_L.$$ This напряженность equal $$E_R = 2\pi \sigma_R k \left( 1 - \frac{L}{\sqrt{R^2+L^2}} \right).$$ Напряженность field, создаваемого lateral wall cylinder find as $$E_L = - \left. \frac{d \varphi}{d z} \right|_0.$$ This напряженность equal $$E_L = 2\pi \sigma_L k \left( 1 - \frac{R}{\sqrt{R^2+L^2}} \right).$$ Total field equal zero, hence obtain: $$\frac{\sigma_{L}}{\sigma_{R}}=-\frac{\sqrt{R^{2}+L^{2}}-L}{\sqrt{R^{2}+L^{2}}-R}=-\frac{41-40}{41-9}=-\frac{1}{32}$$

Answer: $$\frac{\sigma_{L}}{\sigma_{R}}=-\frac{\sqrt{R^{2}+L^{2}}-L}{\sqrt{R^{2}+L^{2}}-R}=-\frac{1}{32}$$

С3Under the conditions of the previous paragraph, the particle was placed in the equilibrium position closest to the cylinder, its mass $m$. Determine the angular frequency $\omega$ of small vibrations of the particle.1.00

Select a point with coordinate $z=0$. We expand the field strength and find that at this point $E(z) = - \alpha z$, $\alpha=2\pi \sigma_0 k \cdot \frac{560}{2809} \frac{z}{b}$. From the vibration equation $$m \ddot{z} = - q \alpha z$$ we get the answer.

Answer: $$\omega^2 = \frac{560 \sigma_0}{5618 \varepsilon_0 b m}$$
