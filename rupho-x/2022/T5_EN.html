Champagne foam

X22 (2022) — English translation

If a bottle is filled with a liquid with a gas dissolved inside, after opening the bottle, the gas will begin to evaporate from the solution and bubbles will form in the liquid. We will study their behavior and estimate the number of bubbles that can form on the surface of a liquid poured into a vessel. You may need the following information: Liquid density $\rho = 1.0 \cdot 10^3 ~\frac{\text{kg}}{\text{м}^3}$; liquid surface tension coefficient $\gamma = 0.05$ N/m; throughout the problem the temperature can be considered constant and equal to $T = 300~ \text{К}$; fluid viscosity $\eta = 1.63 \cdot 10^{-3}~\text{Па}\cdot \text{с}$; atmospheric pressure $P_0 = 10^5$ Pa; universal gas constant $R_G = 8.3 ~\frac{\text{Дж}}{\text{моль} \cdot \text{К}}$; Boltzmann constant $k_B = 1.38 \cdot 10^{-23}~ \text{Дж} / \text{К}$.

Part A. Gas equilibrium in solution

The molar concentration of a saturated gas solution $c$ and its partial pressure above the liquid $P$ are related by Henry’s law $c = k_H P$. For carbon dioxide in the conditions of the problem, we can consider $$ k_H = 3.64 \cdot 10^{-4} ~\frac{\text{моль}}{\text{м}^3 \cdot \text{Па}} $$

In the future in the problem, we will need to study the diffusion of gas dissolved in a liquid. The phenomenon of diffusion is that if the concentration $n$ of molecules in one region of the gas is higher than in the neighboring one, a flow of molecules will arise from the region with a higher concentration. Let the concentration depend on one of the coordinates, for example $z$. Then the flow of molecules in the direction of the $z$ axis (the average number of molecules passing per unit time through a unit area perpendicular to the $z$ axis) will be equal to $$ j = -B \frac{dn}{dz}, $$ где постоянная $B$ – коэффициент диффузии ($\text{m}^2/\text{s}$). Для оценки коэффициента диффузии можно использовать следующую идею. Если на молекулу в веществе действует постоянная сила $F$, то она движется со средней скоростью $v = \beta F$, где коэффициент $\beta$ (подвижность), можно оценить, считая, что на молекулу действует вязкая сила, описывающаяся формулой Стокса $F = 6 \pi \eta rv$. Молекулу можно считать шаром радиусом радиуса $r \approx 10^{-10}~\text{m}$. Если молекулы находятся в поле с потенциальной энергией $U = - F z$, ($z$ – координата) и на каждую из них действует сила $F$ в направлении оси $z$, то из термодинамики известно, что в равновесном состоянии концентрация молекул будет меняться как $$n = n_0 e^{- U/k_BT}.$$ В равновесии поток частиц, возникающий за счет диффузии, должен компенсироваться потоком, создаваемым за счет действия силы $F$.

Part B: Bubble Growth

Let a bubble form on a defect on the glass surface, which has the form of a cylindrical depression, the diameter of which is much less than the depth. While the bubble is on the surface, it looks like part of a ball, and the angle between the surface of the ball and the glass at the connection point is $\theta$. We denote the molar concentration of carbon dioxide in a liquid by $c$. In addition, there are two more characteristic molar concentrations: $c_i$ - the concentration of carbon dioxide at which the solution is in equilibrium with carbon dioxide, the pressure of which is equal to atmospheric pressure. $c_n$ – carbon dioxide concentration at which bubbles stop forming. These concentrations are related by the inequalities $c> c_n > c_i$.

Fig. 1.

The radius of the bubble increases due to the diffusion of carbon dioxide, and the rate of increase in the amount of carbon dioxide substance in the bubble has the form $$ \frac{d\nu}{dt} = k S(c -c_i) $$ Коэффициент $k = B /\kappa D $, где $B$ – коэффициент диффузии, $D$ – диаметр пузыря, $\kappa \approx 0.25$ – некоторый численный коэффициент. Величина $\kappa D$ – характерная длина, на которой меняется концентрация. Площадь поверхности участка сферы $S = f_1(\theta)D^2$, объем $V = f_2(\theta) D^3$.

Part C: Bubble Rising

In this part, we will look at the movement of rising bubbles and estimate the total number of bubbles that can form on the surface of a liquid. The liquid is poured into a cylindrical vessel, the volume of the liquid is $V_L$. The density of the liquid is $\rho_L$. Throughout this part, the influence of surface tension and the liquid column on the pressure inside the bubble can be neglected.

C1
The rate at which a bubble floats up is determined by the force of viscous friction acting on it, which can be calculated using the Stokes formula $F = 6 \pi \eta aU$, where $a$ is the radius of the bubble, $U$ is the velocity of the bubble, $\eta$ is the viscosity of the liquid. Find the rising speed of a bubble of radius $a$.
0.40

When the bubble floats, its radius increases due to carbon dioxide entering from the liquid. After lifting off the surface, it looks like a ball. The pressure inside the bubble $P$ and the temperature $T$ can be considered constant. Consider that the number of moles that enter the bubble per unit time is directly proportional to the area $S$ of the surface of the bubble $d\nu/dt =K \Delta c S$. Here $\Delta c$ is the difference in molar concentrations away from the bubble and on its surface, and the concentration on the surface is such that the gas dissolved in the liquid is in equilibrium with the gas in the bubble. The coefficient $K$ can be expressed through the diffusion coefficient $B$ and the bubble parameters $$ K = 0.63 \frac{B^{2/3} U^{1/3}}{a^{2/3}}. $$
