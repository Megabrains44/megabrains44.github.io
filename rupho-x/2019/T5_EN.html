Note: When a body of mass $m$ (for example, a layer of air) moves with speed $v$ in a rotating frame of reference (rotating with angular speed $\Omega$), it is acted upon by a certain fictitious force, which is called the Coriolis force: $$F_C=2m\Omega v\sin\varphi $$
