Parabolic mirror

X19 (2019) — English translation

A vertical cylindrical vessel with radius $R$ is filled with mercury and rotates around the cylinder axis with a constant angular velocity $\omega$. The surface of the liquid is a paraboloid of revolution, and the cross section is given by the relation $$z=\cfrac{\omega^2r^2}{2g} $$ где $z$ – координата вдоль оси цилиндра (за ноль принимается низшая точка поверхности ртути), $r$ – расстояние до оси $z$, $g$ – the acceleration of free fall.

An observer looks at the surface of mercury from some point on the $z$ axis. He sees the image of his eye. The cylinder is stopped and the surface of the mercury becomes flat. The observer, without changing his position, again sees the image of his eye. The image turned out to be the same size as before, and it did not flip over.

A1
Find the distance $h$ between the observer's eye and the bottom point of the paraboloid when the vessel was rotating.
