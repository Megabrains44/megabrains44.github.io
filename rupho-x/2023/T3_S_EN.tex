\begin{solcard}{A1}{Obtain an exact expression for the angular velocity of orbital rotation $\omega$. Express your answer in terms of $G, m, M$ and $L$.}{0.70}

Let $\vec{e}_1$ be a unit vector drawn towards the smaller body from the center of the larger one. Acceleration of bodies due to gravity: $$\vec{a}_m=-\cfrac{GM}{L^2}\vec{e}_1\quad \vec{a}_M=\cfrac{Gm}{L^2}\vec{e}_1; \quad {\vec{a}_M-\vec{a}_m=\cfrac{G(M+m)}{L^3}\vec{e}_1}. $$ On the other hand: $$\vec{a}_M-\vec{a}_m=\omega^2L\vec{e}_1, $$ from where finally:

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$\omega=\sqrt{\cfrac{G(M+m)}{L^3}} $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{A2}{Obtain an exact expression for the potential difference of the gravitational field of a small body $\Delta{\varphi}_\text{g—Ä}=\varphi_{\text{g—Ä}P}-\varphi_{\text{g—Ä}O}$ at points $P$ and $O$. Express your answer using $G$, $m$, $L$, $\theta$ and $r(\theta)$.}{0.20}

The field potential of a small body at a point whose distance from it is $L_m$ is equal to: $$\varphi_\text{g—Ä}=-\cfrac{Gm}{L_m} $$ Let us find distance –ĺ—ā –ľ–į–Ľ–ĺ–≥–ĺ —ā–Ķ–Ľ–į –ī–ĺ —ā–ĺ—á–ļ–ł $A$ –Ņ–ĺ —ā–Ķ–ĺ—Ä–Ķ–ľ–Ķ –ļ–ĺ—Ā–ł–Ĺ—É—Ā–ĺ–≤: $$L^2_m=L^2+r^2(\theta)-2Lr(\theta)\cos\theta $$ Finally we have:

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$\Delta \varphi_{\text{g—Ä}}=Gm\left(\cfrac{1}{L}-\cfrac{1}{\sqrt{L^2+r^2-2rL\cos\theta}}\right) $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{A3}{Obtain an exact expression for the potential difference of inertial forces $\Delta{\varphi}_\text{–ł–Ĺ}=\varphi_{\text{–ł–Ĺ}P}-\varphi_{\text{–ł–Ĺ}O}$ at points $P$ and $O$. Express your answer using $G$, $m$, $M$, $L$, $\theta$ and $r(\theta)$.}{0.50}

Since the fluid is stationary, and the larger body rotates with a constant angular velocity, the fields of inertial forces $-\Delta{m}\vec{a}_0$ and $\Delta{m}\omega^2\vec{r}_\perp$, acting on a particle of mass $\Delta{m}$, contribute to the potential difference. For the potential difference between points $A$ and $O$ we have: $$\varphi_{\text{–ł–Ĺ}A}-\varphi_{\text{–ł–Ĺ}O}=-\int\limits_{O}^{A}(\omega^2\vec{r}_\perp-\vec{a}_0)\cdot d\vec{r} $$ Taking into account that $\vec{a}_0=Gm\vec{e}_1/L^2$, –į $\vec{r}_\perp=\vec{r}$: $$\varphi_{\text{–ł–Ĺ}A}-\varphi_{\text{–ł–Ĺ}O}=-\int\limits_{O}^{A}(\omega^2\vec{r}-\cfrac{Gm}{L^2}\vec{e}_1)\cdot d\vec{r}=\cfrac{Gmr\cos\theta}{L^2}-\cfrac{\omega^2r^2}{2} $$ Finally:

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbackslash{}textbf{Answer:} $$\Delta\varphi_{\text{–ł–Ĺ}}=\cfrac{Gmr\cos\theta}{L^2}-\cfrac{G(M+m)r^2}{2L^3} $$
\end{solcard}
