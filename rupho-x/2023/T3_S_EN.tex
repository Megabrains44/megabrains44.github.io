\begin{solcard}{A1}{Obtain an exact expression for the angular velocity of orbital rotation $\omega$. Express your answer in terms of $G, m, M$ and $L$.}{0.70}

Let $\vec{e}_1$ be a unit vector drawn towards the smaller body from the center of the larger one. Acceleration of bodies due to gravity: $$\vec{a}_m=-\cfrac{GM}{L^2}\vec{e}_1\quad \vec{a}_M=\cfrac{Gm}{L^2}\vec{e}_1; \quad {\vec{a}_M-\vec{a}_m=\cfrac{G(M+m)}{L^3}\vec{e}_1}. $$ On the other hand: $$\vec{a}_M-\vec{a}_m=\omega^2L\vec{e}_1, $$ from where finally:

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} $$\omega=\sqrt{\cfrac{G(M+m)}{L^3}} $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{A2}{Obtain an exact expression for the potential difference of the gravitational field of a small body $\Delta{\varphi}_\text{gr}=\varphi_{\text{gr}P}-\varphi_{\text{gr}O}$ at points $P$ and $O$. Express your answer using $G$, $m$, $L$, $\theta$ and $r(\theta)$.}{0.20}

The field potential of a small body at a point whose distance from it is $L_m$ is equal to: $$\varphi_\text{gr}=-\cfrac{Gm}{L_m} $$ Find distance from small body to point $A$ by theorem cosine: $$L^2_m=L^2+r^2(\theta)-2Lr(\theta)\cos\theta $$ Finally we have:

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} $$\Delta \varphi_{\text{gr}}=Gm\left(\cfrac{1}{L}-\cfrac{1}{\sqrt{L^2+r^2-2rL\cos\theta}}\right) $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{A3}{Obtain an exact expression for the potential difference of inertial forces $\Delta{\varphi}_\text{i}=\varphi_{\text{i}P}-\varphi_{\text{i}O}$ at points $P$ and $O$. Express your answer using $G$, $m$, $M$, $L$, $\theta$ and $r(\theta)$.}{0.50}

Since the fluid is stationary, and the larger body rotates with a constant angular velocity, the fields of inertial forces $-\Delta{m}\vec{a}_0$ and $\Delta{m}\omega^2\vec{r}_\perp$, acting on a particle of mass $\Delta{m}$, contribute to the potential difference. For the potential difference between points $A$ and $O$ we have: $$\varphi_{\text{i}A}-\varphi_{\text{i}O}=-\int\limits_{O}^{A}(\omega^2\vec{r}_\perp-\vec{a}_0)\cdot d\vec{r} $$ Taking into account that $\vec{a}_0=Gm\vec{e}_1/L^2$, and $\vec{r}_\perp=\vec{r}$: $$\varphi_{\text{i}A}-\varphi_{\text{i}O}=-\int\limits_{O}^{A}(\omega^2\vec{r}-\cfrac{Gm}{L^2}\vec{e}_1)\cdot d\vec{r}=\cfrac{Gmr\cos\theta}{L^2}-\cfrac{\omega^2r^2}{2} $$ Finally:

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} $$\Delta\varphi_\text{i}=\cfrac{Gmr\cos\theta}{L^2}-\cfrac{G(M+m)r^2}{2L^3} $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{B1}{Show that the tangential component of the force $F_\tau(t,\, \theta)$ acting on a particle of mass $\Delta{m}$ located on the surface of a larger body at an angle $\theta$ can be represented as: $$F_\tau(t,\,\theta)=\Delta{m}\alpha\sin(2\omega_1t-2\theta) $$ Find $\alpha$. Answer express through $G$, $m$, $L$ and $r(\theta)$. Note: use result, obtain for solution part $\mathrm{A4}$.}{0.50}

Note: Use the results obtained when solving item $\mathrm{A4}$.


In point $\mathrm{A4}$ we obtained an approximate expression for the potential of inertial forces and the gravitational field of a smaller body: $$\varphi_\text{ext}=-\cfrac{G(M+3m\cos^2\theta)r^2}{2L^3} $$ Then for component force $F_\tau$ obtain: $$F_\tau=-\cfrac{\Delta{m}}{r}\cfrac{\partial{\varphi_\text{ext}}}{\partial{\theta}}=-\cfrac{3Gm\Delta{m}r\sin2\theta}{2L^3} $$ Note, that this component force arise frodue to inhomogeneity gravitational field small body. Since $\omega\neq{\Omega}$, in arbitrary moment time $t$ parameter $\theta$ equal: $$\theta=\theta_0-\omega_1 t, \quad \omega_1 = \omega - \Omega. $$ Also account for, that with required we accuracy $r \approx R$. Then finally have: $$F_\tau(t{,}\theta_0)=\cfrac{3Gm\Delta{m}R}{2L^3}\sin\left(2\omega_1 t-2\theta_0\right) $$

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} $$ \alpha = \frac{3 GmR}{2 L^3}. $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{B2}{Show that the dependence $\theta(t{,}\theta_0)$ has the following form: $$\theta(t)=\theta_0+A\sin(2\omega_1t-2\theta_0-\varphi_0) $$ Find $A$ and $\varphi_0$.  Answer express through $G$, $m$, $L$, $\gamma$,  $\omega_0$, $\omega$, $\Omega$ and $\theta_0$.}{0.80}

Let's substitute $F_\tau(t{,}\theta)$ into the equation of motion: $$\Delta{m}r^2\left(\ddot{\theta}+2\gamma\dot{\theta}+\omega^2_0(\theta-\theta_0)\right)=rF_\tau(t{,}\theta)=\Delta{m}r^2\cdot\cfrac{3Gm}{2L^3}\sin\left(2\omega_1 t-2\theta\right) $$ Since $\omega^2_0\gg{Gm/L^3}$ - one can consider/count, that $F_\tau(t{,}\theta)\approx{F_\tau(t{,}\theta_0)}$. Then: $$\ddot{\theta}+2\gamma\dot{\theta}+\omega^2_0(\theta-\theta_0)=\cfrac{3Gm}{2L^3}\sin\left(2\omega_1 t-2\theta_0\right) $$ Solution equation be seek in form $\theta(t) =\theta_0 + Re\left(\hat{A}e^{2i(\omega-\Omega)t}\right)$: $$\left(\omega^2_0-4\omega_1^2+4i\omega_1\gamma\right)\hat{A}=\cfrac{3Gm}{2L^3}e^{-i(2\theta_0+\pi/2)} $$ We can represent $\hat{A}$ in trigonometric form $\hat{A}=Ae^{-i\varphi_0}$, where:

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} $$A=\cfrac{3Gm}{2L^3\sqrt{(\omega^2_0-4\omega_1^2)^2+16\gamma^2\omega_1^2)}};\qquad \varphi_0=\arctan\cfrac{4\omega_1\gamma}{\omega^2_0-4\omega_1^2}. $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{B3}{Obtain an expression for the rate of growth of height $\dot{h'}(t{,}\,\theta_0)$ at time $t$ at angle $\theta_0$. Express your answer using $h_0$ and $\frac{d\dot{\theta}}{d\theta _0}$}{0.40}

The volume of liquid flowing through one of the faces $hdz$ during time $dt$ is equal to the product of the flow velocity $\dot{\theta} R$ by the area $hdz$ and the considered time $dt$: $$ dV_1 = \dot{\theta} R h dz dt \approx \dot{\theta} R h_0 dz dt. $$ Here we account for, that height surface water change small, and this practically not affects on flux/flow water. Velocity flow, correspond angle $\theta_0$ and $\theta_0 + d \theta_0$ somewhat differ, therefore difference inflowing and follow from volume water $$ dV = \dot{\theta}(\theta_0) R h_0 dz dt -\dot{\theta}(\theta_0+ d\theta_0) R h_0 dz dt = -Rh_0 \frac{\partial \dot{\theta}}{\partial \theta_0}d\theta_0 dz dt. $$ Frodue to this change height surface liquid: $$ dV = Rd\theta_0 dz dh = R d\theta_0 dz \dot{h} dt = -Rh_0 \frac{\partial \dot{\theta}}{\partial \theta_0}d\theta_0 dz. $$ Hence $\dot{h} = - h_0\dfrac{\partial \dot{\theta}}{\partial \theta_0}$, and since $h_0 = const$

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} $$\dot{h'}(t{,}\theta)=-h_0\cfrac{\partial\dot{\theta}(t{,}\theta)}{\partial{\theta}_0}. $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{B4}{Assuming that the amplitude of oscillations $h'$ is the same for all values of $\theta_0$, you obtain the dependence $h(t)$. Express your answer using $h_0$, $A$, $\omega_1$, $\varphi_1$ and $t$.}{0.30}

\begin{solcard}{B6}{Find the moment of force $M_z$ acting from the small body on the surface of the large body relative to the axis $z$. Express your answer using $G$, $m$, $\rho$, $R$, $h_0$, $L$, $A$ and $\varphi_0$.}{1.40}

The force arm corresponding to the angle $\beta$ is equal to $R\sin\beta$. Then for the moment element $dM_z$ we have: $$dM_z=R\sin\beta dF_\tau(t{,}\theta{,}\beta) $$ For $dF_\tau$ also have: $$dF_\tau=\cfrac{3GmR\sin\beta}{2L^3} \Delta m \sin(2\omega_1t-2\theta). $$ Mass consider volume $$ \Delta m = \rho dV = \rho h(t,\, \theta,\, \beta) dS = \rho h(t,\, \theta,\, \beta) R^2 \sin \beta d\beta d\theta. $$ Then obtain: $$dF_\tau=\cfrac{3GmR\sin\beta}{2L^3}  \sin(2\omega_1t-2\theta) \rho h(t,\, \theta,\, \beta) R^2 \sin \beta d\beta d\theta. $$ For $h(t{, }\theta{,}\beta)$ have: $$h(t,\,\theta,\,\beta)=h_0(1+2A\cos(2\omega_1t-\varphi_1)) $$ since $A$ not depend from distance to axis $z$. We can represent expression for $M_z$: $$M_z=\cfrac{3Gm\rho R^4h_0}{2L^3}\int\limits_0^{\pi}\sin^3\beta d\beta\int\limits_0^{2\pi}(1+2A\cos(2\omega_1t-\varphi_1))\sin(2\omega_1t-2\theta)d\theta $$ For the integral by $\beta$ we have: $$\int\limits_0^{\pi}\sin^3\beta d\beta=\int\limits_{-1}^{1}(1-\cos^2\beta)d\cos\beta=\cos\beta-\cfrac{\cos^3\beta}{3}\bigg|_{-1}^{1}=\cfrac{4}{3} $$ Therefore: $$M_z=\cfrac{2Gm\rho R^4h_0}{L^3}\int\limits_0^{2\pi}(1+2A\cos(2\omega_1t-\varphi_1))\sin(2\omega_1t-2\theta)d\theta $$ The second integral is taken over period, therefore for the function $\sin(2 \omega_1t-2\theta)$ it equals zero. Let us pass to the last integral: $$\int\limits_0^{2\pi}\cos(2\omega_1t-\varphi_1)\sin(2\omega_1t-2\theta)d\theta=\int\limits_0^{2\pi}\cos(2\omega_1t-2\theta-\varphi_0)\sin(2\omega_1t-2\theta)d\theta $$ We will use trigonometric formula: $$\sin\alpha\cos\beta=\cfrac{\sin(\alpha+\beta)+\sin(\alpha-\beta)}{2} $$ We obtain: $$M_z=\cfrac{2Gm\rho R^4h_0A}{L^3}\int\limits_0^{2\pi}[\sin(4\omega_1t-4\theta-\varphi_0)+\sin\varphi_0]d\theta $$ Final:

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} $$M_z=\cfrac{4\pi Gm\rho R^4h_0A\sin\varphi_0}{L^3} $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{C1}{Find the orbital angular velocity $\omega_\text{sync}$ and the distance $L_\text{sync}$ between the Earth and the Moon during synchronous rotation. Express the answers in terms of $m$, $M$, $R$, $G$, $L_0$, $\Omega_0$ and find their numerical values. Note: with synchronous rotation, the angular momentum of the Earth associated with rotation around its axis can be neglected.}{0.80}

Note: with synchronous rotation, the angular momentum of the Earth associated with rotation around its axis can be neglected.


Let us use the law of conservation of angular momentum, which we denote as $K$: $$K=I\Omega_0+\cfrac{mML^2_0\omega_0}{M+m}=const $$ where $I=2MR^2/5$ - moment of inertia Earth's sphere about the diameter. In the established regime $\Omega=\omega=\omega_\text{sync}$, therefore: $$K=\omega_\text{sync}\left(I+\cfrac{mML^2_\text{sync}}{m+M}\right) $$ Since for synchronous rotation Earth can be considered as a material point: $$K\approx{\cfrac{mML^2_\text{sync}\omega_\text{sync}}{m+M}} $$ Use relation $$\omega^2=\cfrac{G(M+m)}{L^3} $$ obtain: $$K\approx\cfrac{G^{2/3}Mm}{(m+M)^{1/3}\omega^{1/3}_\text{sync}} $$ from where finally:

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} $$\omega_\text{sync}\approx\cfrac{G^2m^3M^3}{(M+m)\left(\cfrac{2MR^2\Omega_0}{5}+\cfrac{mM}{m+M}\sqrt{G(M+m)L_0}\right)^3}\approx{1{,}376\cdot{10^{-6}}~\text{s}^{-1}} L_\text{sync}=\cfrac{(m+M)\left(\cfrac{2MR^2\Omega_0}{5}+\cfrac{mM}{m+M}\sqrt{G(M+m)L_0}\right)^2}{Gm^2M^2}\approx 5{,}971\cdot{10^8}~\text{m} $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{C2}{The distance between the Earth and the Moon is increasing at a rate of $\dot{L}_{0}=1~\text{cm}/\text{year}$. Find the value of the average angular acceleration of the Earth $\dot{\Omega}_0$. Express the answer in terms of $m$, $M$, $R$, $G$, $L_0$, $\Omega_0$, $\dot{L}_0$ and find its numerical value.}{0.70}

The angular momentum of the system $K$ using the connection $\omega$ and $L$ is reduced to the form: $$K=I\Omega+\cfrac{mML^2}{m+M}\sqrt{\cfrac{G(m+M)}{L^3}} $$ Differentiate, find: $$I\dot{\Omega}+\cfrac{mM}{2(m+M))}\sqrt{\cfrac{G(m+M)}{L}}\dot{L}=0 $$ from where:

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} $$\dot{\Omega}_0=-\cfrac{5m\dot{L}_0}{4(m+M)R^2}\sqrt{\cfrac{G(m+M)}{L_0}}\approx-1{,}22\cdot{10^{-22}}~\text{s}^{-1} $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{C3}{Find the numerical values of $\omega_0$ and $\gamma$.}{0.80}

At point $\mathrm{B5}$ it was found that the position of the tide lags behind the Moon by an angle $\varphi_0/2$ in the direction of rotation of the Moon relative to the Earth. Then we have: $$2\beta=\arctan\cfrac{-4\omega_1\gamma}{\omega^2_0-4\omega^2_1}=-\varphi_0 $$ Also for difference height of the tide and ebb we have: $$\Delta{h}=h_\text{high}-h_\text{low}=4Ah_0 $$ or, after substitution expression for $A$: $$\Delta{h}=\cfrac{6Gmh_0}{L^3_0\sqrt{(\omega^2_0-4\omega^2_1)^2+16\omega^2_1\gamma^2}}=\cfrac{6Gmh_0\cos 2\beta}{L^3_0(\omega^2_0-4\omega^2_1)} $$ We find:

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} $$\omega_0=\sqrt{4\omega^2_1+\cfrac{6Gmh_0\cos 2\beta}{L^3_0\Delta{h}}}\approx 1{,}664\cdot{10^{-4}}~\text{s}^{-1}\qquad \gamma=\cfrac{3Gmh_0\sin 2\beta}{2(-\omega_1)L^3_0\Delta{h}}\approx 3{,}025\cdot{10^{-6}}~\text{s}^{-1} $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{C5}{Estimate the numerical value of $\tau_2$. Express your answer in years.}{0.70}
