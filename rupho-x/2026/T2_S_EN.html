A1An infinite uniformly charged thin thread is in a vacuum. The thread is located along the $z$ axis, the linear charge density is $\kappa$. Determine the dependence of the electrostatic potential of this thread $\varphi(r)$ in the entire space up to a constant. Express your answer using $\varepsilon_0$, $\kappa$, $r$.0.40

From symmetry, the electric field is directed radially. Then its value can be found from Gauss’s theorem: $$ E(r)=\dfrac{\kappa}{2\pi\varepsilon_0 r}. $$Integrating, we get expression for potentialа: $$ \varphi(r)=-\int\limits_{a}^{r} E(r) dr =-\dfrac{\kappa}{2\pi\varepsilon_0 }\ln{\frac{r}{a}}. $$Here $a$ is the distance from which the potential is measured.

Answer: $$ \varphi(r)=-\dfrac{\kappa}{2\pi\varepsilon_0 }\ln{\frac{r}{a}} $$
