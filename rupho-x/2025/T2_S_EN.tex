\begin{solcard}{A3}{Obtain expressions for the components of the vector $d \vec{L} = (dL_x, dL_y, dL_z)$ in the coordinate system $OXYZ$. Express your answer using $X$, $Y$, $Z$, $\Omega_x$, $\Omega_y$, $\Omega_z$, $dm$.}{0.30}

Let's expand the double vector product in the expression for \(d\vec{L}\): $$ d\vec{L} =dm (\vec{r}  \times  (\vec{\Omega} \times \vec{r})) = dm (\vec{\Omega}r^2 - \vec{r}(\vec{\Omega} \cdot \vec{r})). $$Let us write the obtained vector expression component-by-component: $$ dL_x = dm\left(\Omega_x(X^2 + Y^2 + Z^2) - X(\Omega_xX + \Omega_y Y + \Omega_z Z)\right), \\ dL_y = dm\left(\Omega_y(X^2 + Y^2 + Z^2) - Y(\Omega_xX + \Omega_y Y + \Omega_z Z)\right), \\ dL_z = dm\left(\Omega_z(X^2 + Y^2 + Z^2) - Z(\Omega_xX + \Omega_y Y + \Omega_z Z)\right). $$ Simplifying, we get the answer.

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} $$ dL_x = dm\left(\Omega_x(Y^2 + Z^2) - \Omega_y XY - \Omega_z XZ \right)  \\ dL_y = dm\left( - \Omega_x YX + \Omega_y(X^2 + Z^2) - \Omega_z YZ \right)  \\ dL_z = dm\left( - \Omega_x ZX - \Omega_y ZY + \Omega_z(X^2 + Y^2)\right) $$

Taking into account initial condition total energy equal zero. $$ \frac{A\dot{\alpha}^2}{2} + \frac{C^2 \omega_0^2}{2A} \alpha^2  -mgl\alpha = 0 $$ Such form expression for energy correspond oscillation with frequency $$\omega = \frac{C}{A} \omega_0,$$ amplitude $$\alpha_0 = \frac{Amgl}{C^2 \omega_0^2}$$ and displacement from center $$\alpha_1 = \alpha_0 = \frac{Amgl}{C^2 \omega_0^2}.$$Taking into account all find parameter motion and initial condition obtain dependence $\alpha(t)$.

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} $$ \alpha(t) = \frac{Amgl}{C^2 \omega_0^2} \left(1 - \cos \left(\frac{C}{A}\omega_0 t\right)\right) $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{D9}{Find the dependence of the precession angle on time $\varphi(t)$. At the initial moment $\varphi(0) = \varphi_0$. Express your answer using $A$, $C$, $m$, $g$, $l$, $\omega_0$, $\varphi_0$.}{0.50}

Let's express $\dot{\varphi}(t)$ using the found dependence $\alpha(t)$: $$ \dot{\varphi} = \frac{L_1 - L_2\cos{\theta}}{A\sin^2{\theta}} = \frac{C\omega_0\sin{\alpha}}{A\cos^2{\alpha}} \simeq \frac{mgl}{C\omega_0}\left(1 - \cos \left(\frac{C}{A}\omega_0 t\right)\right) $$ Integrating, we get the answer.

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} $$ \varphi(t) = \varphi_0 + \frac{mgl}{C\omega_0} \left(t - \frac{A}{C\omega_0} \sin \left(\frac{C}{A} \omega_0 t\right)\right) $$
\end{solcard}
