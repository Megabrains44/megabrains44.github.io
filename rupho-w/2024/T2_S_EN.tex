\begin{solcard}{A1}{Since without a section of the circuit between nodes $A$ and $B$ the electrical circuit consists entirely of constant voltage sources and resistors, the dependence of the current $I_{AB}$ flowing from node $A$ to node $B$ on the voltage $U_{AB}=\varphi_A-\varphi_B$ in this section of the circuit can be represented in a form that does not depend on the element connected between nodes $A$ and $B$: $$I_{AB}=\cfrac{\mathcal{E}_0-U_{AB}}{r_0}{.} $$Determine $\mathcal{E}_0$ and $r_0$. Answer express through $\mathcal{E}$ and $R$.}{1.50}

If an ideal voltmeter is connected between nodes $A$ and $B$, the voltage across it is $$ U_V = \mathcal{E}\left( \frac{6R}{6R + 4 R} - \frac{2 R}{2 R + 8 R}\right) = \frac{2}{5} \mathcal{E} = \mathcal{E}_0. $$ If voltage on source equal zero, it one can replace on wire with zero resistance. Then  diagram between derivation $A$ and $B$ be represent itself two pair параллельно соединенных resistor, $(2R,\, 8R)$ and $(6R,\, 4R)$, соединенных sequentially, and means resistance $$ r_0 = R_{AB} = \frac{2R \cdot 8R}{2 R + 8 R} + \frac{6R \cdot 4R}{6R + 4 R} = 4R. $$Then dependence voltage from force current be have form $$ U_{AB} = \mathcal{E}_0 - I_{AB} r_0, $$from which $$ I_{AB} = \frac{\mathcal{E}_0 - U_{AB}}{r_0}. $$Альтернативно one can calculate current short замыкания $I_c = \mathcal{E}_0/r_0$, which be течь through ideal амперметр, подключенный between derivation $A$ and $B$. For such подключении general resistance chain, to which подключен source $\mathcal{E}$, equal $$ r_1 = \frac{2 R \cdot 6 R}{2 R + 6 R} + \frac{8 R \cdot 4 R}{8R + 4 R} = \frac{25}{6} R. $$Then total current through source $\mathcal{E}$ $I_\mathcal{E} = 6\mathcal{E}/25 R$, and current through resistor $2R$ and $8 R$ is equal to correspondingly: $$ I_1 = \frac{3}{4} I_\mathcal{E} = \frac{9\mathcal{E}}{50 R}, \quad I_2 = \frac{1}{3}  I_\mathcal{E}  = \frac{2\mathcal{E}}{25 R}. $$Then current short замыкания $$ I_c = I_{AB} = I_1 - I_2 = \frac{\mathcal{E}}{10 R}. $$Hence we get прежнее value resistance $$ r_0 = \mathcal{E}_0/I_c = 4 R. $$

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbf{Answer:} $$r_0 = 4 R, \quad \mathcal{E}_0 = \frac{2}{5} \mathcal{E} $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{A2}{Obtain the dependence of the current strength $I_{AB}$ on the value $\mathcal{E}$. Express your answer using $\mathcal{E}$, $R$, $U_0$ and $I_0$.}{0.50}

Suppose that the voltage on the nonlinear element is less than $U_0$, then this voltage can be found from the equation $$ (r_0 + R) I_{AB} = \mathcal{E}_0 - U_{AB}, \quad (r_0 + R) I_0 \frac{U_{AB}}{U_0} = \mathcal{E}_0 - U_{AB}, $$from which $$ U_{AB} =  \frac{\mathcal{E}_0 U_0}{U_0 + (r_0 + R) I_0}, \quad \mathcal{E}_0 \le U_0 + (r_0 +R) I_0. $$Correspond value current $$ I_{AB} =  \frac{\mathcal{E}_0 I_0}{U_0 + 5 R I_0}, \quad \mathcal{E}_0 \le U_0 + 5R I_0. $$For further magnification voltage source voltage on nonlinear element станет larger $U_0$, and current through it is equal to $I_0$.  Voltage on nonlinear element $U_{AB} = \mathcal{E}_0 - I_0 r_0$. Substitute expression for $\mathcal{E}_0$ and $r_0$, we get the answer.

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbf{Answer:} $$ I_{AB} = \begin{cases} \frac{2 \mathcal{E} I_0}{5 (U_0 + 5R I_0)},  \quad \mathcal{E} \le \frac{5}{2} \left(U_0  + 5 R I_0 \right);\\ I_0, \quad \mathcal{E} > \frac{5}{2} \left(U_0  + 5 R I_0 \right). \end{cases} $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{B1}{Obtain the dependence of the current $I_C(t)$ through the capacitor on time $t$. Express your answer in terms of $\mathcal{E}$, $R$, $U_0$, $I_0$, $C$ and $t$.}{0.80}

Then the current through the capacitor

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbf{Answer:} $$ I_C = \frac{\mathcal{E}}{R + U_0/I_0} e^{- \frac{t}{C(R + U_0/I_0)}} $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{B2}{What amount of heat $Q_\text{н.э}$ will be released on the nonlinear element during the establishment of constant current? Express your answer in terms of $\mathcal{E}$, $R$, $U_0$, $I_0$ and $C$. Find the numerical value.}{0.60}

When charging a capacitor, a charge $q = C\mathcal{E}$ passes through the source, and it does work $A_{ист}  = q \mathcal{E} = C \mathcal{E}^2$. This work goes to increase the energy of the capacitor and the heat released: $$ A_{ист}  = \frac{C \mathcal{E}^2}{2} + Q, $$therefore in chain выделяется heat $Q = C \mathcal{E}^2/2$. It is divided between the resistor and the nonlinear element in proportion to their resistances (since at the voltages in question the nonlinear element behaves like a resistor)


$$ Q_{\text{н.э.}} = \frac{r_0}{r_0 + R} \frac{C \mathcal{E}^2}{2} = \frac{U_0}{U_0 + R I_0} \frac{C \mathcal{E}^2}{2} = 0.9~\mathrm{mJ} $$

\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{B3}{After what time $\tau$ the amount of heat released on the nonlinear element will reach $80\%$ from the value $Q_\text{н.э}$? Express your answer in terms of $R$, $U_0$, $I_0$, and $C$. Find the numerical value.}{0.60}

The power on the nonlinear element depending on time has the form $$ P = I_C^2 U_0/I_0 = A e^{- 2t/t_0}, \quad A = \frac{\mathcal{E}^2 U_0 I_0}{(R I_0 + U_0)^2}, \quad t_0 = C(R + U_0/I_0). $$Then over time $t$ on nonlinear element выделяется heat $$ Q(t) = \int_0 ^t P(t) dt = A \frac{t_0}{2} \left( 1 - e^{- 2t/t_0}\right) = Q_{\text{н.э.}} \left( 1 - e^{- 2t/t_0}\right). $$in moment time, when $Q = 0.8 Q_{\text{AD}}$, $e^{-2t/t_0} = 0.2$,

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbf{Answer:} $$ t = \frac{\ln 5}{2} t_0 =  \frac{\ln 5}{2} C \left(R + \frac{U_0}{I_0}\right) = 0.0804~\mathrm{s} $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{B4}{Obtain the dependence of the current $I_C(t)$ through the capacitor on time $t$. Express your answer in terms of $\mathcal{E}$, $R$, $U_0$, $I_0$, $C$ and $t$.}{1.50}

Now the initial voltage on the nonlinear element is greater than $U_0$, which means the current through the nonlinear element (equal to the current through the capacitor) is equal to $I_C = I_0$. This current will remain constant until the voltage across the capacitor drops to $U_0$. Up to this point, the charge of the capacitor depends on time as $q = I_0 t$, which means the voltage on the nonlinear element is $$ U_{\text{н.э.}}  = \mathcal{E} - R I_0 -I_0 t/C. $$This dependence справедлива, пока $U_{\text{AD}} \ge U_0$, that is $$ t \le t_1 = \frac{C}{I_0} (\mathcal{E}   - U_0 - R I_0 ). $$Further current be экспоненциально затухать, as for обычной зарядке capacitor: $$ I = I_0 \exp\left(- \dfrac{t- t_1}{C(R + U_0/I_0)}\right). $$

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbf{Answer:} $$ I_C = \begin{cases} I_0, \quad t \le \frac{C}{I_0} (\mathcal{E} - U_0 - R I_0 );\\ I_0 \exp\left(- \dfrac{t- C(\mathcal{E}-U_0 - R I_0)/I_0}{C(R + U_0/I_0)}\right), \quad t >\frac{C}{I_0} (\mathcal{E}  -U_0- R I_0 ). \end{cases} $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{B5}{What amount of heat $Q_\text{н.э}$ will be released on the nonlinear element during the establishment of constant current?  Find the numerical value with an accuracy of at least $1\%$.}{1.50}

While the current remains constant, the power on the nonlinear element $$ P = I_0 U_{\text{н.э.}} = I_0 (\mathcal{E}  - R I_0 -I_0 t/C). $$Then over time $t_1$ выделится heat $$ Q_1 = \int_0^{t_1} dt I_0 (\mathcal{E}  - R I_0 -I_0 t/C) = I_0 (\mathcal{E} - R I_0) t_1 - I_0 ^2t_1^2/2C, $$$$ Q_1 = C(\mathcal{E} - R I_0)(\mathcal{E} -U_0- R I_0) -  C(\mathcal{E}-U_0 - R I_0)^2/2 = \frac{C}{2} \left( (\mathcal{E} - I_0 R)^2  - U_0^2\right) = 1.2~\mathrm{mJ} $$Further dependence power from time have form ($t' = t - t_1$) $$ P = \frac{U_0}{I_0} I_0^2 e^{- 2t'/t_0}, \quad t_0 = C (R + U_0/I_0) $$and means total выделившееся heat $$ Q_2 = \int_0 ^\infty dt' U_0 I_0  e^{- 2t'/t_0} = \frac{1}{2} U_0 I_0 t_0 = \frac{1}{2} U_0 I_0 C (R + U_0/I_0) = 2.5~\mathrm{mJ} $$

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbf{Answer:} $$Q = Q_1 + Q_2  = \frac{C}{2} \left( (\mathcal{E} - I_0 R)^2  - U_0^2\right) + \frac{C}{2} U_0 (U_0 + I_0 R)  =  \frac{C}{2} \left((\mathcal{E} - I_0 R)^2 + U_0 I_0 R \right) = 3.7~\mathrm{mJ}$$
\end{solcard}
