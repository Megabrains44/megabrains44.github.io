\begin{solcard}{A1}{Determine the speed $v$ and acceleration $a$ of the left end of the rope at the moment when the left end is lowered by the amount $x$. Express your answer using $g$, $L_1$, $L$, $\alpha$ and $x$.}{1.50}

First solution: The length of the part of the rope hanging on the right is $L_2=L_1+L\sin\alpha$, and the total length of the rope is: $$l=2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha){.} $$ Using the law conservation mechanical energy: $$\cfrac{mv^2}{2}=-\Delta{W}_p{,}$$ where $m=\lambda l$ ‚Äì mass rope, and $\Delta{W}_p$ ‚Äì change –µ—ë potential energy in field —Ç—è–∂–µ—Å—Ç–∏. Change potential energy rope in field —Ç—è–∂–µ—Å—Ç–∏ equal change potential energy section rope length $x$, coordinate $x_C$ of the center of mass which –∏–∑–º–µ–Ω–∏–ª–∞—Å—å from value $x_{C_1}=-x/2$ to $ x_{C_2}=x/2$. Hence have: $$\Delta{W}_p=-\lambda x\cdot g\cdot(x_{C_2}-x_{C_1})=-\lambda gx^2{.} $$ Substitute $\Delta{W}_p$ in conservation of mechanical energy, obtain: $$\cfrac{mv^2}{2}=\cfrac{\lambda lv^2}{2}=\lambda gx^2\Rightarrow v=x\sqrt{\cfrac{2g}{l}}{,} $$ from:


$$l=2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha){.} $$


Let's use the law of conservation of mechanical energy:


$$\cfrac{mv^2}{2}=-\Delta{W}_p{,}$$


where $m=\lambda l$ is the mass of the rope, and $\Delta{W}_p$ is the change in its potential energy in the gravitational field.


The change in the potential energy of the rope in the gravitational field is equal to the change in the potential energy of a section of the rope of length $x$, the coordinate $x_C$ of the center of mass of which has changed from the value $x_{C_1}=-x/2$ to $x_{C_2}=x/2$. From here we have:


$$\Delta{W}_p=-\lambda x\cdot g\cdot(x_{C_2}-x_{C_1})=-\lambda gx^2{.} $$


Substituting $\Delta{W}_p$ into the law of conservation of mechanical energy, we obtain:


$$\cfrac{mv^2}{2}=\cfrac{\lambda lv^2}{2}=\lambda gx^2\Rightarrow v=x\sqrt{\cfrac{2g}{l}}{,} $$


where:


Since the speed $v(x)$ and the displacement $x$ are related by direct proportionality: $$a=v\sqrt{\cfrac{2g}{l}}=\cfrac{2gx}{l}{,} $$from where:


Second solution: Let's write down the equation of motion for each section of the rope: $$\lambda(L_1+x)a=\lambda(L_1+x)g-T_1\qquad \lambda La=T_1-T_2+\lambda gL\sin\alpha\qquad \lambda (L_2-x)a=T_2-\lambda (L_2-x)g{,} $$ where $T_1$ and $T_2$ ‚Äì force tension rope near block $1$ and $2$ correspondingly. Add equation, obtain: $$\lambda(L_1+L_2+L)a=\lambda g(L_1+L\sin\alpha-L_2+2x){,} $$ from where


$$\lambda(L_1+x)a=\lambda(L_1+x)g-T_1\qquad \lambda La=T_1-T_2+\lambda gL\sin\alpha\qquad \lambda (L_2-x)a=T_2-\lambda (L_2-x)g{,} $$


where $T_1$ and $T_2$ are the rope tension forces near blocks $1$ and $2$, respectively.


Adding the equations, we get:


$$\lambda(L_1+L_2+L)a=\lambda g(L_1+L\sin\alpha-L_2+2x){,} $$


where


Multiplying the expression for acceleration by speed, we get: $$av=\cfrac{d(v^2/2)}{dt}=\cfrac{2gxv}{2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)}=\cfrac{g}{2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)}\cfrac{d(x^2)}{dt} $$Integrate, obtain: $$\cfrac{v^2}{2}=\cfrac{gx^2}{2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)}{,} $$from:

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbf{Answer:} $$v=x\sqrt{\cfrac{2g}{2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)}}{.} $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{A2}{Determine the amount of displacement of the left end of the rope $x_1$ at the moment in time when the tension of the section of the rope lying on the inclined plane is constant along the length of this section. Express your answer in terms of $L_1$, $L$ and $\alpha$.  At what ratios $L_1$, $L$ and $\alpha$ is the value $x_1$ less than the initial length of the right section of the rope?}{0.80}

Let us introduce the axis $y$, directed along the inclined plane from block $1$ to block $2$. Let us write in projection onto the axis $y$ Newton‚Äôs second law for a section of rope of length $dy$ lying on an inclined plane: $$dT+\lambda g\sin\alpha dy=\lambda ady{.} $$Quantity $dT=0$ for $a(x_1)=g\sin\alpha$. Hence: $$\cfrac{2gx_1}{l}=g\sin\alpha\Rightarrow x_1=\cfrac{l\sin\alpha}{2}{,} $$from where:


Let's compare the value $x_1$ with the value $L_2$: $$x_1=L_1\sin\alpha+\cfrac{L\sin\alpha(1+\sin\alpha)}{2}\qquad L_2=L_1+L\sin\alpha{.} $$Coefficient before $L_1$ and $L$ in expression for $L_2$ larger corresponding coefficient in expression for $x_1$, therefore:

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbf{Answer:} $$x_1=L_1\sin\alpha+\cfrac{L\sin\alpha(1+\sin\alpha)}{2}{.} $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{A3}{Determine the maximum tension force of the rope at the moment of time when its left end has shifted by a distance $x$. Express your answer in terms of $\lambda$, $g$, $L_1$, $L$, $\alpha$ and $x$.}{1.50}

On vertical sections of the rope, the greatest tension force is achieved at the points of contact of the blocks, since the acceleration $a(x)$ is always less than $g$. Let us denote by $T_1$ and $T_2$ the tension force of the rope at the points of contact of blocks $1$ and $2$, respectively. From Newton's second law for the left vertical section of the rope we obtain: $$\lambda(L_1+x)g-T_1=\lambda(L_1+x)a\qquad T_2-\lambda(L_2-x)g=\lambda(L_2-x)a{,} $$from which: $$T_1=\lambda(L_1+x)(g-a)\qquad T_2=\lambda(L_2-x)(g+a){.} $$–ü—Ä–æ–∞–Ω–∞–ª–∏–∑–∏—Ä—É–µ–º inclined section rope. From solution part $\mathrm{A2}$ follow: $$\cfrac{dT}{dy}=\lambda(a-g\sin\alpha) {.} $$For $x\leq x_1$ quantity $dT/dy$ not positive, and for $x\geq x_1$ ‚Äì –Ω–µ–æ—Ç—Ä–∏—Ü–∞—Ç–µ–ª—å–Ω–∞, from which follow, that: $$T_{max}=\begin{cases} T_1\quad\text{for}\quad x\geq x_1\\ T_2\quad\text{for}\quad x\leq x_1 \end{cases} $$Substitute expression for $a$, $L_2$ and $x_1$, we get:

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbf{Answer:} $$T_{max}=\begin{cases} \cfrac{\lambda g(L_1+x)(2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)-2x)}{2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)}\quad \text{for}\quad x\geq L_1\sin\alpha+\cfrac{L\sin\alpha(1+\sin\alpha)}{2}\\ \cfrac{\lambda g(L_1+L\sin\alpha-x)(2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)+2x)}{2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)}\quad \text{for}\quad x\leq L_1\sin\alpha+\cfrac{L\sin\alpha(1+\sin\alpha)}{2} \end{cases} $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{A4}{Determine the displacement values ‚Äã‚Äã$x_{{max}(1)}$ and $x_{{max}(2)}$ of the left end of the rope, at which it begins to lose contact with blocks $1$ and $2$, respectively, assuming that contact with the other block is maintained. Since radius block –º–∞–ª ‚Äì condition –æ—Ç—Ä—ã–≤–∞ following: $$T=\lambda v^2{.} $$–í—Ä–µ–º–µ–Ω–Ω–æ –ø–µ—Ä–µ–æ–±–æ–∑–Ω–∞—á–∏–º quantity $x_{max (1)}$ and $x_{max(2)}$  over $x_1$ and $x_2$ correspondingly. Equation for $x_1$ following: $$\cfrac{\lambda g(L_1+x_1)(2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)-2x_1)}{2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)}=\cfrac{2\lambda gx^2_1}{2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)}{.} $$from which: $$4x^2_1-x_1L(1+\sin\alpha)-L_1(2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha))=0{.} $$–£ This quadratic equation has one positive root, therefore:


The equation for $x_2$ is: $$\cfrac{\lambda g(L_1+L\sin\alpha-x_2)(2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)+2x_2)}{2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)}=\cfrac{2\lambda gx^2_2}{2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)}{,} $$from which: $$4x^2_2+x_2L(1-\sin\alpha)-(L_1+L\sin\alpha)(2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha))=0{.} $$This quadratic equation has one positive root, so:

\begin{solcard}{B1}{Determine the displacement value $x_0$ of the left end of the rope corresponding to the equilibrium position of the system. Express your answer in terms of $m$, $\lambda$, $L_1$, $L$, $\alpha$, $g$, $k$ and $F_0$.}{0.80}

Let's write down the equilibrium conditions for three sections of the rope: $$T_1=\lambda g(L_1+x_0)+mg\qquad T_2-T_1=\lambda gL\sin\alpha\qquad T_2=\lambda g(L_2-x_0)+F_\text{—É–ø—Ä}{,} $$from which: $$F_\text{—É–ø—Ä}=\lambda g(L\sin\alpha+L_1+x_0+x_0-L_1-L\sin\alpha)+mg=mg+2\lambda gx_0{.} $$Since $F_\text{control}=F_0+kx_0$, we get:

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbf{Answer:} $$x_0=\cfrac{mg-F_0}{k-2\lambda g}{.} $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{B2}{The equilibrium position from the previous paragraph exists if the spring stiffness exceeds some minimum value $k_{min}$. Identify $k_{min}$. The denominator of the result obtained in point $\mathrm{B1}$ must be positive, so we have the limitation: $$k>2\lambda g{.} $$Quantity $x_0$ also –æ–≥—Ä–∞–Ω–∏—á–µ–Ω–∞ length $L_2$ right vertical section rope, therefore: $$x_0=\cfrac{mg-F_0}{k-2\lambda g}\leq L_1+L\sin\alpha\Rightarrow k\geq 2\lambda g+\cfrac{mg-F_0}{L_1+L\sin\alpha}{.} $$The last limitation is more significant, so we have:

\begin{solcard}{B3}{Find the change in the potential energy of the system $\Delta{W}_p$ when the left end of the rope is displaced by the amount $x$. Express your answer in terms of $m$, $\lambda$, $L_1$, $L$, $\alpha$, $g$, $k$, $F_0$ and $x$.}{0.80}

For quantities $\Delta{W}_{p(\text{g})}$ and $\Delta{W}_{p(\text{in})}$ we have: $$\Delta{W}_{p(\text{g})}=-mgx\qquad \Delta{W}_{p(\text{g})}=-\lambda gx^2{.} $$Change potential energy deformation spring $\Delta{W}_{p(\text{def})}$ equal area under graph $F_\text{control}(\Delta{l})$ and constitute: $$\Delta{W}_{p(\text{def})}=F_0x+\cfrac{kx^2}{2}{.} $$Finally:

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbf{Answer:} $$\Delta{W}_p=\cfrac{(k-2\lambda g)x^2}{2}-(mg-F_0)x{.} $$
\end{solcard}

\begin{solcard}{B4}{If we take the potential energy $W_p$ at the equilibrium position equal to zero, then it can be represented in the form $$W_p=\cfrac{A(x-B)^2}{2}. $$Determine $A$ and $B$. Answer express through $m$, $\lambda$, $L_1$, $L$, $\alpha$, $g$, $k$ and $F_0$.}{0.70}

From the expression for $\Delta{W}_p$ we have: $$W_p=\cfrac{(k-2\lambda g)x^2}{2}-(mg-F_0)x+C=\cfrac{k-2\lambda g}{2}\left(x-\cfrac{mg-F_0}{k-2\lambda g}\right)^2-\cfrac{(mg-F_0)^2}{2(k-2\lambda g)}+C{.} $$Determine potential energy in position equilibrium equal zero: $$W_p=\cfrac{k-2\lambda g}{2}\left(x-\cfrac{mg-F_0}{k-2\lambda g}\right)^2{.} $$Thus:

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbf{Answer:} $$A=k-2\lambda g\qquad B=\cfrac{mg-F_0}{k-2\lambda g}{.} $$
\end{solcard}

The maximum speed of the load is achieved at the point with coordinate $x_0$ and is determined from the law of conservation of mechanical energy: $$\cfrac{(m+\lambda(2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha))v^2_{max}}{2}=\cfrac{Ax^2_0}{2}{,} $$from where:

\par\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Answer:} \textbf{Answer:} $$v_{max}=\cfrac{mg-F_0}{k-2\lambda g}\sqrt{\cfrac{k-2\lambda g}{m+\lambda(2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)}}{.} $$
