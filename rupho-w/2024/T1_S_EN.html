A1Determine the speed $v$ and acceleration $a$ of the left end of the rope at the moment when the left end is lowered by the amount $x$. Express your answer using $g$, $L_1$, $L$, $\alpha$ and $x$.1.50

First solution: The length of the part of the rope hanging on the right is $L_2=L_1+L\sin\alpha$, and the total length of the rope is: $$l=2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha){.} $$ Using the law conservation mechanical energy: $$\cfrac{mv^2}{2}=-\Delta{W}_p{,}$$ where $m=\lambda l$ – mass rope, and/but $\Delta{W}_p$ – change её potential energy in field тяжести. Change potential energy rope in field тяжести equal change potential energy section rope length $x$, coordinate $x_C$ of the center of mass which изменилась from value $x_{C_1}=-x/2$ to $ x_{C_2}=x/2$. Hence have: $$\Delta{W}_p=-\lambda x\cdot g\cdot(x_{C_2}-x_{C_1})=-\lambda gx^2{.} $$ Substitute $\Delta{W}_p$ in conservation of mechanical energy, obtain: $$\cfrac{mv^2}{2}=\cfrac{\lambda lv^2}{2}=\lambda gx^2\Rightarrow v=x\sqrt{\cfrac{2g}{l}}{,} $$ from:

$$l=2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha){.} $$

Let's use the law of conservation of mechanical energy:

$$\cfrac{mv^2}{2}=-\Delta{W}_p{,}$$

where $m=\lambda l$ is the mass of the rope, and $\Delta{W}_p$ is the change in its potential energy in the gravitational field.

The change in the potential energy of the rope in the gravitational field is equal to the change in the potential energy of a section of the rope of length $x$, the coordinate $x_C$ of the center of mass of which has changed from the value $x_{C_1}=-x/2$ to $x_{C_2}=x/2$. From here we have:

$$\Delta{W}_p=-\lambda x\cdot g\cdot(x_{C_2}-x_{C_1})=-\lambda gx^2{.} $$

Substituting $\Delta{W}_p$ into the law of conservation of mechanical energy, we obtain:

$$\cfrac{mv^2}{2}=\cfrac{\lambda lv^2}{2}=\lambda gx^2\Rightarrow v=x\sqrt{\cfrac{2g}{l}}{,} $$

where:

Since the speed $v(x)$ and the displacement $x$ are related by direct proportionality: $$a=v\sqrt{\cfrac{2g}{l}}=\cfrac{2gx}{l}{,} $$from where:

Second solution: Let's write down the equation of motion for each section of the rope: $$\lambda(L_1+x)a=\lambda(L_1+x)g-T_1\qquad \lambda La=T_1-T_2+\lambda gL\sin\alpha\qquad \lambda (L_2-x)a=T_2-\lambda (L_2-x)g{,} $$ where $T_1$ and $T_2$ – force tension rope near block $1$ and $2$ correspondingly. Add equation, obtain: $$\lambda(L_1+L_2+L)a=\lambda g(L_1+L\sin\alpha-L_2+2x){,} $$ from where

$$\lambda(L_1+x)a=\lambda(L_1+x)g-T_1\qquad \lambda La=T_1-T_2+\lambda gL\sin\alpha\qquad \lambda (L_2-x)a=T_2-\lambda (L_2-x)g{,} $$

where $T_1$ and $T_2$ are the rope tension forces near blocks $1$ and $2$, respectively.

Adding the equations, we get:

$$\lambda(L_1+L_2+L)a=\lambda g(L_1+L\sin\alpha-L_2+2x){,} $$

where

Multiplying the expression for acceleration by speed, we get: $$av=\cfrac{d(v^2/2)}{dt}=\cfrac{2gxv}{2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)}=\cfrac{g}{2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)}\cfrac{d(x^2)}{dt} $$Integrate, obtain: $$\cfrac{v^2}{2}=\cfrac{gx^2}{2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)}{,} $$from:

Answer: $$v=x\sqrt{\cfrac{2g}{2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)}}{.} $$

A3Determine the maximum tension force of the rope at the moment of time when its left end has shifted by a distance $x$. Express your answer in terms of $\lambda$, $g$, $L_1$, $L$, $\alpha$ and $x$.1.50

On vertical sections of the rope, the greatest tension force is achieved at the points of contact of the blocks, since the acceleration $a(x)$ is always less than $g$. Let us denote by $T_1$ and $T_2$ the tension force of the rope at the points of contact of blocks $1$ and $2$, respectively. From Newton's second law for the left vertical section of the rope we obtain: $$\lambda(L_1+x)g-T_1=\lambda(L_1+x)a\qquad T_2-\lambda(L_2-x)g=\lambda(L_2-x)a{,} $$from which: $$T_1=\lambda(L_1+x)(g-a)\qquad T_2=\lambda(L_2-x)(g+a){.} $$Проанализируем inclined section rope. From solution part $\mathrm{A2}$ follow: $$\cfrac{dT}{dy}=\lambda(a-g\sin\alpha) {.} $$For $x\leq x_1$ quantity $dT/dy$ not positive, and/but for $x\geq x_1$ – неотрицательна, from which follow, that: $$T_{max}=\begin{cases} T_1\quad\text{for}\quad x\geq x_1\\ T_2\quad\text{for}\quad x\leq x_1 \end{cases} $$Substitute expression for $a$, $L_2$ and $x_1$, we get:

Answer: $$T_{max}=\begin{cases} \cfrac{\lambda g(L_1+x)(2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)-2x)}{2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)}\quad \text{for}\quad x\geq L_1\sin\alpha+\cfrac{L\sin\alpha(1+\sin\alpha)}{2}\\ \cfrac{\lambda g(L_1+L\sin\alpha-x)(2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)+2x)}{2L_1+L(1+\sin\alpha)}\quad \text{for}\quad x\leq L_1\sin\alpha+\cfrac{L\sin\alpha(1+\sin\alpha)}{2} \end{cases} $$

B1Determine the displacement value $x_0$ of the left end of the rope corresponding to the equilibrium position of the system. Express your answer in terms of $m$, $\lambda$, $L_1$, $L$, $\alpha$, $g$, $k$ and $F_0$.0.80

Let's write down the equilibrium conditions for three sections of the rope: $$T_1=\lambda g(L_1+x_0)+mg\qquad T_2-T_1=\lambda gL\sin\alpha\qquad T_2=\lambda g(L_2-x_0)+F_\text{упр}{,} $$from which: $$F_\text{упр}=\lambda g(L\sin\alpha+L_1+x_0+x_0-L_1-L\sin\alpha)+mg=mg+2\lambda gx_0{.} $$Since $F_\text{control}=F_0+kx_0$, we get:

Answer: $$x_0=\cfrac{mg-F_0}{k-2\lambda g}{.} $$

B3Find the change in the potential energy of the system $\Delta{W}_p$ when the left end of the rope is displaced by the amount $x$. Express your answer in terms of $m$, $\lambda$, $L_1$, $L$, $\alpha$, $g$, $k$, $F_0$ and $x$.0.80
