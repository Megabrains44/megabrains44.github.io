Rolling and sliding friction --- Solution

A1Determine experimentally the rolling friction coefficient $k$ of a cylinder rolling on a wooden block. Describe the measurement method and sketch the installation diagram.11.00

Let's balance the ruler on the edge of the block, thereby determining the position of the center of mass of the ruler: $x_r=248~\mathrm{mm}$. Using the ruler as a lever, we determine the mass ratio $M/m$ of the cylinder and the ruler $$M/m=3.44±0.22.$$ Fix/attach ruler as rod on base cylinder use adhesive mass. Side ruler with scale direct in side cylinder: so convenient determine position ruler relatively cylinder. On edge table fix/attach sheet with draw line under different angle to vertical. Launch oscillation system cylinder and rod. Quantity amplitude record by coincidence with line on sheet. Line on sheet not necessarily draw as ray from one center: one can select position so, so that line coincide with inclined ruler for correspond position cylinder. Energy system for oscillation, expressed through angular amplitude $\varphi$: $$W=mgd(1-cos⁡\varphi )≈mgd \varphi^2/2.$$ For rotation cylinder with rod on angle $d\alpha$ torque reaction support perform work: $$\delta A=-M⋅d\alpha=-k(m+M)g⋅d\alpha.$$ Remaining force work not perform. Notice, that even when system be located in position equilibrium, but motion still not end, system still equal be characterized angular amplitude $\varphi$. Change energy system for rotation on small angle $d \alpha$: $$dW=mgd⋅\varphi d\varphi=-k(m+M)g⋅d\alpha.$$ Decrease energy over 1/4 period (here sign «$\Delta$» mean decrease, and/but not change): $$mgd⋅\varphi⋅\Delta\varphi_{T/4} = k(m+M)g⋅\varphi.$$ Over $N$ period quantity decrease amplitude be in $4N$ times larger. Express coefficient of friction rolling $$k=\frac{m}{m+M} \frac{d⋅\Delta\varphi_N}{4N}.$$

Using adhesive paste, attach the ruler as a rod to the base of the cylinder. Let's place this system on a block. Let's start tilting the block (Fig. 1). At a certain angle of inclination $\beta$ of the block to the horizon, the system will begin to slide. Friction coefficient $$ \mu = \mathrm{tg}~ ⁡\beta=\mathrm{tg}~⁡ (17° \pm 1°)= 0,31 \pm 0,02 .$$
